## 1. The DeepSeek moment: the end of the supremacy narrative

**January 20, 2025** (the day of Trump's inauguration): DeepSeek releases **R1**, an **open-weights** reasoning model (MIT license) at parity with OpenAI o1 (AIME 2024: 79.8; MATH-500: 97.3). The first open frontier model published by a Chinese company. It becomes the most-downloaded open-weights model on Hugging Face (~10.9M downloads).

**January 27, 2025**: the shock wipes out **$589 billion of NVIDIA market capitalization in a single session** (-16.9%), the largest one-day loss in stock-market history.

**The cost controversy**, often distorted in both directions: the figure of **$294,000** (published in *Nature*, peer-reviewed, 512 H800 GPUs) covers only R1's RL phase; the V3 base model cost ~$5.6-6M (2.79M GPU-hours). Actual total cost ≈ **$5.9M**: not "free," but in no way comparable to the hundreds of millions spent by US labs.

**Why this is central to the dossier**: the DeepSeek moment empirically invalidates the axiom shared by the e/acc camp (Part 1) AND the hawks (Part 17), according to which the compute lock guarantees American supremacy. It also feeds the internal critique: Jensen Huang would go on to call the export controls a "failure" (Part 16).

## 2. The real state of the gap in 2026

- The gap has shrunk from "years" to "months." The best Chinese model remains ~9 benchmark points behind the top OpenAI/Anthropic/Google models, but matches or beats them on code and math, at **15 to 30× lower cost** (DeepSeek V3.2 ≈ $0.28/M tokens vs. GPT-5.2 ≈ $10/M). *(Trend corroborated by USCC and PIIE; fine-grained figures from commercial sources, medium confidence.)*
- **Qwen (Alibaba): 700M downloads as of January 2026**, overtaking Llama in cumulative downloads as early as October 2025. Chinese providers' share on OpenRouter: from under 2% (early 2025) to more than 45% of weekly volume (April 2026). But the general public remains on US apps (OpenAI ~800M users, Gemini ~750M, vs. Doubao/Qwen ~100M).
- **The "Two Loops" thesis (USCC report, March 2026)**: the Chinese open-weights strategy is not generosity, it is an industrial strategy; an a16z partner estimates that ~80% of US startups are building on Chinese base models. Openness creates dependency.
- **The censorship is in the weights**: DeepSeek, Qwen, GLM, and Kimi align their answers on Taiwan, Tiananmen, Xinjiang. Article 4 of the Interim Measures mandates adherence to "core socialist values." When 80% of Western startups build on these models, the question of what is embedded in the weights becomes geopolitical.

## 3. State ideology: "community of destiny" and the AI+ plan

The Chinese ideological architecture is the exact structural inverse of the one in this dossier (private oligarchs capturing the state):

- **Global AI Governance Initiative** (Xi, October 2023): AI attached to the "community of common destiny for mankind," avowed multilateralism, central role for the UN.
- **July 26, 2025** (the day after Trump's AI Action Plan): Li Qiang unveils at WAIC a 13-point Action Plan and proposes a **World AI Cooperation Organization (WAICO)** based in Shanghai, relaunched by Xi at APEC in November 2025.
- **The "AI+" plan** (State Council directive, August 26, 2025): diffusion of AI across 6 sectors; targets for 2027 (extensive integration), 2030 ("intelligent economy"), 2035 ("socialist modernization"). With an explicit paragraph: "strengthen our capacity to use AI to maintain and shape national security."
- **Military-civil fusion (MCF)**: steered by the Central Science and Technology Commission chaired by Xi; civilian innovation structurally feeds military power.
- **The "gray rhino" speech** (Ding Xuexiang, the regime's no. 6, Davos, January 21, 2025): a "chaotic competition" over AI will give rise to a highly probable but neglected risk; metaphors of Ali Baba's cave and Pandora's box, "driving fast without brakes"; an appeal to the UN. **The paradox the dossier must face head-on: in 2025-2026, it is Beijing that speaks the language of multilateral caution Washington has abandoned** (cf. Part 17, Vance's Paris speech three weeks later).

## 4. Governance: Beijing regulates its labs harder than Washington

| Date | Text | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Jan. 2023 | Deep Synthesis Provisions | The world's first deepfake rules |
| Aug. 15, 2023 | Interim Measures on Generative AI (CAC + 6 agencies) | The world's first binding regulation of generative AI; "core socialist values" mandatory |
| March 14, 2025 (in force Sept. 1, 2025) | AI Content Labeling Measures | The first national mandatory regime for identifying synthetic media (explicit labels + watermark in metadata) |

Compare: the United States of 2025-2026 repealed the Biden EO, renamed its Safety Institute, attempted a moratorium on state laws (Part 17). **The asymmetry is real but its meaning is ambivalent**: Chinese regulation is first and foremost an instrument of political content control (censorship), not a protection of citizens against the state. It does prove, however, that a state *can* constrain its national champions when it wants to, the "regulation is impossible" argument is refuted by the most advanced country after the US.

- **Summits**: China signs at Bletchley (2023), does NOT sign the Seoul declaration (2024), signs at Paris (February 2025) the "inclusive and sustainable AI" declaration that the US and UK refuse. Constant diplomatic escalation (vice-minister at Bletchley → vice-premier at Paris). No documented support for a binding treaty.
- **Signals**: Xi and Biden agree (November 2024) that AI must not control nuclear weapons; the July 2024 plenum lists AI risks alongside biorisks.

## 5. The Chinese "caution camp" exists

A structuring discovery for Part 11 (which treated the caution camp as purely Western):

- **CnAISDA** (China AI Safety and Development Association, launched at the Paris summit in February 2025, formalized June 2025): the Chinese equivalent of the AI Safety Institutes, aggregating Tsinghua, BAAI, CAICT, Shanghai AI Lab. Stated objective: "early warning thresholds for AI systems with catastrophic or existential risk." Relationship with the state "deliberately ambiguous" (Fu Ying's own admission).
- **Andrew Yao (Yao Qizhi)**, Turing Award winner, Tsinghua, co-leads CnAISDA: "we have suddenly found a way to create a new species far more powerful than ourselves."
- **Yi Zeng** (Chinese Academy of Sciences, UN AI advisory group): signatory of the March 2023 "pause" letter and of the statement on extinction risk.
- **Xue Lan** (I-AIIG, Tsinghua): the theorist of AI governance on the Chinese side.

**An acknowledged zone of uncertainty**: sincerity or image diplomacy? The sources do not settle it. But the very existence of a caution pole backed by the Chinese state breaks the dossier's implicit geography (caution = the West, acceleration = the rest of the world).

## 6. The thinkers: Yuk Hui, Zhao Tingyang

Two Sinophone thinkers, **independent** (not state ideologues), who displace the debate:

- **Yuk Hui** (Hong Kong, Erasmus Rotterdam): the concepts of **cosmotechnics** and **technodiversity**. There is no ONE universal technology; each cosmology engenders its own relation to technics. His critique strikes both sides: Silicon Valley's universalism AND China's military-civil fusion share the same technological monism. "In Defence of Technodiversity" lecture (Glasgow, March 2025): acceleration and deceleration are the same dead end. Developed in Part 19.
- **Zhao Tingyang** (Chinese Academy of Social Sciences): reinvention of **Tianxia** ("all under heaven"); a June 2025 talk on "the ethical limits of AI" with a provocative inversion of the Western paradigm: aligning AI with human values would be "species suicide," human nature being selfish and cruel. *(Sources: analyses citing his talks in Chinese, no peer-reviewed literature, medium confidence.)*

A notable fact: **no Chinese ideologue espousing a "techno-fascism"** in the dossier's sense has been identified. The state discourse remains "socialist modernization" and "community of destiny"; the functional equivalents of Yarvin or Land have no standing in Beijing.

## 7. Verdict: two concentrations, two architectures

Part 13's grid (Paxton/Eco/Stanley) applied to the two models yields this:

| | **US model 2025-26** | **Chinese model** |
|---|---|---|
| Engine | Private capital capturing the state ("Thielverse") | Party-state subordinating private capital |
| Ideology | e/acc, NRx, longtermism, private, competing | "Socialist modernization," official, single |
| Surveillance | Private (Palantir) sold to the state | State-run (social credit, Xinjiang) imposed on the private sector |
| Regulation of labs | Dismantled (2025-26) | Harsh but oriented toward content control |
| International discourse | "We totally reject global governance of AI" | UN multilateralism, WAICO, "gray rhino" |
| Analytical category | **Oligarchic techno-fascism** (Coeckelbergh) | **State techno-authoritarianism** |

**Three conclusions for the dossier:**
1. **China is not the double of American techno-fascism, it is its inverted mirror**: there, the state swallows tech; here, tech swallows the state. Both converge toward surveillance and concentration, through opposite architectures. Conflating the two (as race rhetoric does) prevents thinking about either.
2. **The Chinese scarecrow is the main rhetorical resource of the American accelerationist camp**: every part of this dossier where "China" appears (Andreessen, Aschenbrenner, Amodei) uses it to shut down the internal democratic debate. Documenting the real China means disarming that use.
3. **Part 11's axis (concentration vs. democratic control) also runs through China**: there exists a caution camp there, critical thinkers, and zero democratic control. The dossier's structuring cleavage is global, not American.
