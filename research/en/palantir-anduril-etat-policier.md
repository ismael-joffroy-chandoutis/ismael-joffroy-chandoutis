## 1. ImmigrationOS: The Operating System of Deportation

In April 2025, ICE awarded Palantir a **$30M** contract to develop **ImmigrationOS**, a platform designed to streamline deportation operations. The agency's justification document is explicit: an "urgent and compelling" need, Palantir is the "only source" capable of delivering in time, because the company is "already ingesting and processing" data from several federal agencies and can deliver a prototype "in less than six months." The prototype was expected by **September 25, 2025**, the contract runs through **September 2027**, and it is carried out in execution of Presidential Executive Orders EO 14159 and EO 13773.

The $30M is not a standalone deal: it was grafted onto the existing ICM (Investigative Case Management) contract, which has ballooned past **$145M**. And the bigger picture is broader: according to public records obtained by the *New York Times*, Palantir has received **more than $900M in federal contracts** since Trump's return.

ImmigrationOS has three declared functions, which must be quoted as they are because they state the project: (1) streamline the identification and apprehension of people prioritized for removal ("violent criminals," gang members, visa overstays); (2) track and report "self-deportations" with "near real-time visibility"; (3) make deportation logistics more efficient. The vocabulary ("violent criminals," MS-13, Tren de Aragua) is the packaging; the capability built is general.

## 2. Data Fusion: ELITE, the Dossier with a Confidence Score

The core of the apparatus is not a camera, it is the **fusion of disparate databases**. Palantir's systems aggregate, regardless of the accuracy of the sources, passport data, Social Security records, IRS tax files, license-plate reads, extractions from seized phones, driver's-license scans, location data, and social media, to produce a unified, exploitable profile of each individual.

In January 2026, *404 Media* revealed a tool named **ELITE** (Enhanced Leads Identification & Targeting for Enforcement): it populates a map of potential deportation targets, opens a dossier on each person, and provides a **"confidence score"** on their current address. According to testimony before an Oregon court, the tool receives addresses from the Department of Health (HHS), which includes **Medicaid** data. The same source reports that ICE now has, through Palantir-powered systems, access to information tied to **roughly 20 million people** (statements by an ICE official, Matthew Elliston, at the Border Security Expo in Phoenix), and that agents access it from field devices.

**Palantir's denial must appear, for fairness and because it is revealing.** The company (blog, January 15, 2026) disputes the EFF's reading: ELITE would serve to surface the likely addresses of "specific" individuals (final orders of removal, serious criminal charges), its role would be limited to data integration, and HHS-ICE sharing would be governed by the Privacy Act. Karp, for his part, settled it in a December interview: "Are we building a database that can be used for surveillance? No." The question is precisely there: a database that fuses tax, health, license plates, and phones of 20 million people *is* that database, whether the declared use is "targeted" or not. During **Operation Metro Surge** (Minnesota), agents used facial recognition and phone surveillance in public, scanning the faces of people who were observing or protesting. The capability, once built, overflows its declared perimeter.

## 3. Flock Safety: The License-Plate Mesh and ICE's Indirect Access

If Palantir is the brain, **Flock Safety** is one of the eyes. The company operates **more than 40,000 automatic license-plate readers (ALPR)** in over 5,000 communities, continuously recording plate, color, make, stickers. An agent can track a vehicle's movements over time, often without a warrant.

The May 2025 revelation (*404 Media*, on records from Danville, Illinois): ICE obtained **indirect** access to the national network by routing its searches through local and state police. More than **4,000 immigration-related lookups** between June 2024 and May 2025, with reasons logged as "immigration," "ICE+ERO," "ICE WARRANT," by agencies across dozens of states. The method circumvents the law: Illinois prohibits the use of ALPR for immigration, and so does Flock's own policy. An audit by Secretary of State Giannoulias (August 2025) confirms that Flock let CBP access Illinois cameras, and orders the cutoff. In June 2025, Flock suspends access in California, Illinois, and Virginia. In Virginia, five counties (Fairfax, Chesterfield, Isle of Wight, Loudoun, Stafford) had conducted ~50 immigration-related searches between June 2024 and April 2025.

The judicial and local counter-fires are numerous: a class action in California (Gibbs Mura / Milberg, February 26, 2026, amended April 3, 2026, for illegal out-of-state sharing); Attorney General Bonta sues the City of El Cajon (October 2025); Secure Justice sues Oakland; a Norfolk (Virginia) judge ruled that Flock's collection constitutes a "search" under the Fourth Amendment. Local refusals: Evanston cuts out-of-state access, Austin does not renew ("Austin should not be participating in Trump's mass surveillance programs," Council Member Mike Siegel), Denver rejects unanimously.

## 4. Anduril: The Autonomous Towers and the Legislative Monopoly

**Anduril** (Palmer Luckey, founder of Oculus, company created in 2017, funded by Thiel's Founders Fund) provides the physical counterpart at the border. Its software product, **Lattice**, fuses by AI the data from heterogeneous sensors (radar, thermal, camera) into a 3D model of the environment. Its hardware, the **Sentry Towers**, are autonomous solar towers of 10 to 25 meters that detect movement by radar, orient a camera, and classify the object by algorithm before even turning on their radio to transmit. Detection range up to 12 km depending on the variant.

A CBP partner since 2019, a program of record in 2020, ~290 towers installed along the Mexican border by 2023. The 2025 turning point is legislative: the **"One Big Beautiful Bill"** (signed July 4, 2025) commits more than $6B to border technology, AND requires that every new tower be certified **"autonomous."** Yet only Anduril's technology holds that CBP certification. The documented result: a **de facto monopoly** on new towers, which locks out the historic competitors (Elbit, General Dynamics).

Anduril's line of defense is aesthetic and worth noting, because it is the same rhetorical operation as at Palantir: "we track objects, not individuals" (Matt Steckman), "this is not facial recognition." Critics respond on the substance: entire towns are scanned, and the deterrence infrastructure pushes migration routes toward deadlier terrain, correlated with an increase in mortality (a GAO report points to failings). The same technology is deployed by the UK Home Office to scan small boats in the Channel.

## 5. Anduril Beyond the Border: The $61 Billion Defense Empire

The border is only a doorway. Anduril's capital trajectory states the scale: $1.5B raised at a $14B valuation (summer 2024), then a Series G of **$2.5B at $30.5B** (June 2025, led by **Founders Fund putting in $1B, the largest check in its history**), then **$5B at $61B** (May 2026, led by Thrive Capital and a16z). Valuation doubled in nine months. A likely IPO in 2026.

The contracts that justify these figures: a **10-year US Army enterprise contract of up to $20B** (March 2026), consolidating 120+ procurement actions; the takeover of Microsoft's augmented-reality headset (IVAS), **up to $22B** for 120,000 headsets; autonomous aircraft with General Atomics (up to $9B); a Navy counter-drone contract ($642M); more than 10,000 low-cost hypersonic missiles. The **Arsenal-1** factory (Ohio, 460,000 m², operational mid-2026, 4,000+ jobs) industrializes the production of autonomous weapons. Revenue ~$1B in 2024 (doubled), projected ~$4.3B in 2026.

A direct link with Part 20: Luckey publicly supported the government's position against Anthropic in the Pentagon dispute. Anthropic's red lines (refusal of mass surveillance and autonomous weapons) are exactly what Anduril sells.

## 6. The Power Structure: Thiel, the Voting Trust, Stephen Miller

The through-line is **Peter Thiel** (Part 2). He co-founded Palantir, and Founders Fund signed the largest check in its history for Anduril. The two pillars of the American surveillance state of 2026 come out of the same portfolio.

Control is locked. At Palantir, the Class F shares are held in a **voting trust** by the three founders (Stephen Cohen, Alex Karp, Peter Thiel), guaranteeing them up to **49.999999% of the voting power**. The company is experiencing one of the most spectacular stock surges on the market (Q4 2025 revenue up 70% to $1.4B, of which +66% on the US government side; Q1 2026 at $1.63B, +85%; a multiple of about 208 times forward earnings), a $1B DHS blanket agreement opening access to CBP/ICE plus TSA/FEMA/Secret Service/CISA, and a role as backbone of the **Golden Dome** missile-defense program ($185B). In May 2026, the executives sold more than $125M in shares (pre-arranged 10b5-1 plans).

The conflict of interest is named: **Stephen Miller**, architect of Trump's immigration policy, holds a substantial financial stake in Palantir. And the ideology is asserted: Palantir published a 22-point "brief" tied to Karp's book *The Technological Republic*, arguing that Silicon Valley owes a "moral debt" to the United States and that economic growth and national security are the ultimate criteria by which a civilization is judged. This is not a company that endures its role, it is a company that theorizes it.

- **The courts and the cities** (section 3): class actions, Fourth Amendment rulings, contract refusals. This is the same repertoire as Part 25: the terrain, the law, local litigation. It does not stop the machine, it slows it down and documents it.

## 8. Verdict: The Infrastructure of a Power That No Longer Needs to Convince

1. **It is the material instantiation of the entire thesis.** The theoretical parts describe a capture of the state by tech capital; here, we touch it: a deportation OS, a confidence score on a person's address, a mesh of 40,000 cameras, a legislative monopoly on border towers, all out of Thiel's portfolio. The vectofascism of Part 24 (a power that modulates without showing itself) has a body of concrete and silicon, and it is called ImmigrationOS and Sentry Tower.
2. **The rhyme with historical fascism is functional, not analogical.** A private apparatus, built to identify, track, and remove a targeted population, with aesthetic denial built in ("objects, not individuals"; "a surveillance database? No"). What was missing from 20th-century fascism (instant filing, the score, total fusion of databases) is here, and it is outsourced to publicly traded companies.
3. **Denial is the mechanism, not the exception.** Palantir disputes ELITE/Medicaid, Anduril says it tracks only objects, Karp says he is not building surveillance. As in Part 24: the system is all the more effective because it can always say "I am not here." The dossier must hold both: cite the denials AND what is independently documented (the contracts, the audits, the congressional letters, the court testimony).
4. **For the techno-fascism grid (Part 13) and the two axes (Part 11)**: Palantir and Anduril are pure axis 2 (maximal concentration, locked founder control, monopoly) in service of the "capture of the state" axis (Parts 9, 17) made operational. This is no longer an ideology to debate, it is an infrastructure to dismantle or regulate. And it is, of the entire dossier, the part that justifies the word "fascism" not by the thought of the actors but by the function of their machines.
