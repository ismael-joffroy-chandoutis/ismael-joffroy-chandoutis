## 1. Elon Musk
**The operational and media arm.**
- Came out of **X.com** (merged into PayPal) → member of the PayPal Mafia.
- Empire: **Tesla, SpaceX, Neuralink, xAI, X (formerly Twitter)**.
- Led **DOGE** (Department of Government Efficiency) under Trump: massive cuts in the federal administration.
- **SF imaginary**: fan of **Iain M. Banks** (the *Culture* — which he nevertheless reads against the grain, Banks being a socialist) and of **Asimov** (*Foundation*). SpaceX names its barges after Banks's ships.
- **Ideological profile**: pragmatic accelerationist, pro-natalism, asserted "free speech" on X, hostility to regulation.
- **Power**: combines **capital + infrastructure + access to the State + media megaphone** — hence his top position in the hierarchy.
- **Friction**: a volatile relationship with Trump; rivalry with Altman/OpenAI (which he co-founded and then left).

## 2. Sam Altman
**The master of frontier AI — outside Thiel's orbit.**
- CEO of **OpenAI** (ChatGPT). Former president of **Y Combinator**.
- **November 2023 crisis**: ousted then reinstated within days (a founding episode in AI governance).
- **Tools for Humanity / World (formerly Worldcoin)**: crypto + **iris scanning** for "proof of humanity"; talks with **Visa** for wallets. Invests in the charter cities **Próspera** and **Praxis**.
- **Profile**: techno-optimist, but **not** in the neo-reactionary camp; a central pivot in the "accelerate vs secure" question.
- **Power**: controls the **most visible AI infrastructure** — a decisive variable that can reshuffle the deck, since it is independent of the Thiel network.
- **2026 context**: the rise of an **anti-AI backlash** (up to violent incidents targeting his house, according to *Fortune*).

## 3. JD Vance
**State relay no. 1.**
- Former **venture capitalist** (the **Narya Capital** fund, backed by **Thiel and Andreessen**).
- **Vice president of the United States.**
- **Yarvin lineage**: as early as 2021, cites Yarvin to propose **"firing every mid-level bureaucrat and replacing them with our own people"** (= Yarvin's "RAGE").
- **2025-2026**: in Munich and at the Paris AI summit, **lambasts Europe** ("the threat comes from within", the retreat of free speech). In April 2026, calls on tech bosses (Musk, Altman, Amodei, Pichai, Nadella) to "control" their technology in the face of safety risks → an ambivalent **techno-populist** stance (between acceleration and protection).
- **Power**: weak in capital/infra, **maximal in State access**.

## 4. Alex Karp
**The philosopher-CEO of surveillance.**
- Co-founder and **CEO of Palantir** (with Thiel).
- An atypical path: a bachelor's degree in **philosophy** (Haverford), a J.D. (Stanford), a **doctorate in social theory at Frankfurt under Jürgen Habermas** (a thesis on aggressiveness and culture).
- Describes himself as a **"socialist and progressive, but not woke"** (2018) — a singular stance in this milieu. Defends an avowed **"pro-Western"** vision.
- Book ***The Technological Republic*** (2025, with N. Zamiska): argues for a reorientation of tech toward the **national/military power** of the West.
- **Net worth** > $18B (2025); Time 100 (2025).
- **Power**: **infrastructure** (Palantir at the core of the "Authoritarian Stack", US Army contract ~$10B); ideologically apart (Habermas, not Yarvin).

## 5. Curtis Yarvin
**The court ideologue (Mencius Moldbug).**
- Born in **1973**, a programmer turned far-right blogger.
- **Unqualified Reservations** (2007-2014) then **Gray Mirror** (2020-): American democracy is a **failure** to be replaced by a **"responsible" monarchy**, modeled on **corporate governance** (a CEO-king).
- Flagship plan: **"RAGE" (Retire All Government Employees)** — a purge of the civil service.
- **Urbit**: the decentralized computing platform he designed; co-founds **Tlon** (2013), returns in 2024 as "wartime CEO" (triggering departures).
- **Influence**: Thiel invested in his startup and calls him a "powerful historian"; a direct inspiration for **Vance**, an echo in **Bannon, Michael Anton**. Linked to **Project 2025** by several analysts.
- **Power**: nil in capital/infra, **very high in ideology** — he supplies the mental software of the seizure of power.

## 6. Balaji Srinivasan
**The theorist of "exit" and the network state.**
- Former **CTO of Coinbase**, former **a16z** partner.
- Book ***The Network State*** (2022): **"voice vs exit"** — building **opt-in digital societies** (backed by the blockchain) that aggregate enough members/capital/territory to aim for **diplomatic recognition**.
- **2024-2025**: launches **The Network School** (an island near Singapore, Forest City); ~120 "startup societies" recorded, funded by Thiel, Andreessen, Altman, Armstrong. Charter cities **Próspera/Praxis**.
- **Objection** project (with Thiel): an AI judging the "accuracy" of journalism via an "Honor Index".
- **Power**: moderate in capital, strong in **ideology** (gives the "product" form to the idea of exiting the nation-state, inherited from *The Sovereign Individual*).
