## 1. Who Dugin Is, and What He Is Not

Aleksandr Dugin is a Russian ideologue born in 1962, often nicknamed "Putin's brain" or "Putin's Rasputin." Both nicknames are media exaggerations worth correcting at the outset: Dugin holds no official position in the Kremlin and his direct influence on Putin is contested by the specialists themselves. His daughter, Darya Dugina, was killed by a car bomb near Moscow in August 2022. He was sanctioned by the United States as early as 2015 for his role in the Ukraine conflict.

What is documented, by contrast, is the influence of his book. According to analyst John B. Dunlop, few works published in post-communist Russia have exerted on the military, police, and diplomatic elites an influence comparable to that of his 1997 "neo-fascist" treatise, *Foundations of Geopolitics*. The book is a cynical repudiation of the architecture of international relations built after 1945, and it proposes a strategy of subjugating the former Soviet states and confronting the West. This is the Dugin who is not shown in the interview.

## 2. The Three Pillars: Traditionalism, Eurasianism, the Fourth Political Theory

Dugin's thought rests on three interlocking columns:

- **Eurasianism.** Russia is not a country but a distinct civilization, neither European nor Asian, destined to resist Western liberal modernity. Geopolitically, it is the opposition of Land (the Eurasian Heartland Empire) to Sea (the Atlanticist thalassocracy, the United States and NATO). Politically, it is **multipolarity**: a world fragmented into great sovereign civilizations, against the liberal universalism of a single world order.
- **The Fourth Political Theory.** An ideology that claims to transcend the three great "isms" of the 20th century (liberalism, communism, fascism) in favor of a paradigm grounded in civilizational identity and the rejection of universalism. Its watchword, which must be quoted because it says everything: "plural anthropology, the rejection of universalism." There is no universal humanity, only incommensurable peoples-civilizations.
- **Traditionalism.** The metaphysical layer, which critiques the "spiritual decline" of the modern world and calls for a return to the sacred, hierarchical values of pre-modernity.

## 3. The Hidden Substrate: Guénon, Evola, Traditionalism

This is where the real bridge lies, and it is deeper than a geopolitical opinion. **Traditionalism** (with a capital T) has nothing to do with the everyday meaning of "conservative." It is an esoteric school born in the early 20th century around **René Guénon** (a Frenchman who moved from Catholicism to Sufism, herald of an aristocratic anti-materialist order) and **Julius Evola** (an Italian occultist and fellow traveler of fascism, who despised bourgeois civilization in the name of the noble and heroic man).

Its postulates: time is cyclical, we are living in the dark age (the Hindu Kali Yuga), modernity is a fall, and progress, liberalism, capitalism, and communism are allied forces destroying the sacred hierarchy. This is exactly Dugin's mental scenery, who reportedly discovered Evola at the Lenin Library in Moscow. And it is the link connecting a Russian ideologue to an American political strategist.

## 4. The Bridge to the American New Right: Bannon, Carlson

The reference work is **Benjamin Teitelbaum's *War for Eternity* (2020)**, based on direct interviews with three ideologues: the American **Steve Bannon**, the Russian **Dugin**, and the Brazilian **Olavo de Carvalho**. Its ambitious thesis: Traditionalism is the most transformative political movement of the early 21st century, with relays at the top of three nations (Russia via Dugin, Brazil via de Carvalho, the United States via Bannon).

The starting point is factual: in January 2017, the *New York Times* revealed that Bannon, then a Trump adviser, had cited Evola in a talk at a Vatican conference in 2014. Teitelbaum shows how Bannon carried out a **"metaphysics of the peasantry"**: he inverted the elitist Traditionalism of Evola and Guénon (where only a sacred caste accesses truth) to make it a populism in which it is the "Middle American" working classes who, through their estrangement from the modern institutions of knowledge, keep contact with pre-modern authenticity. Protectionism, closed borders, and withdrawal from distant wars become the spiritual weapons of an exit from the Kali Yuga.

The mainstream moment is the interview of **Dugin by Tucker Carlson, on April 30, 2024**, recorded during Carlson's trip to Moscow (the same one where he interviewed Putin). Twenty minutes in which Dugin argues that liberalism frees the individual from "any collective identity" and leads to the "loss of human identity," that LGBT is a "necessary element" of it, and that the West loathes Putin not for his military aggression but for his "values." Carlson oversold the guest ("the most famous philosopher in Russia," "Amazon won't sell his books," false), and Dugin relished it: he congratulated himself on Telegram for breaking into the "American mainstream" (6 million views), praised "the libertarian Elon Musk," and called Carlson "the number one journalist in the world." The bridge is not a metaphor: it is a platform, an audience, and a mutual recognition.

## 5. Why This Concerns a Techno-Fascism Dossier

At first glance, Dugin is nothing like a tech man. The connection does not run through code, it runs through **the common enemy and the shape of the desired world**.

- **The common enemy**: liberal universalism, the "globalist" managerial order, mass democracy. This is exactly the target of Andreessen's manifesto (Part 1), of Thiel's Schmitt (Part 2), of Yarvin's "Cathedral" (Part 9). Dugin provides the civilizational and sacred version of what the tech-right formulates in the language of optimization and exit.
- **The shape of the desired world**: Dugin's **multipolarity** (the world shattered into incommensurable sovereign civilizations) is structurally a cousin of Yarvin's **patchwork** and Balaji's **Network State** (Part 4). Both shatter the universal liberal order into sovereign fragments. One calls it the Eurasian Empire, the other the startup-city, but it is the same gesture: doing away with universal humanity in favor of closed, hierarchical units.
- **The shared substrate**: Evola's Traditionalism via Bannon is the soil from which the American new right, including its tech wing (Vance, trained at Thiel's, raised on Yarvin), draws its anti-modernity. The dossier had Marinetti (accelerationist futurism) and Land (dark accelerationism). It was missing the inverse and complementary pole: the **reactionary-Traditionalist**, which supplies the depth of time that accelerationism lacks.

In other words, the American tech-right is traversed by two apparently opposed currents that meet on anti-liberalism: an **accelerationist** current (let's go faster toward the future, e/acc, Land) and a **Traditionalist** current (let's return to the sacred pre-modern order, Evola, Dugin via Bannon). Vance and Thiel stand at the crossing of the two.

## 6. The Caveat: What Must Not Be Over-Read

A fascism dossier discredits itself by overplaying conspiracy. So, honestly:

- **Teitelbaum is contested.** Several reviews judge that he overestimates the role of Traditionalism: Bannon may have read Evola "out of intellectual curiosity," as he read Blavatsky, without making it the metaphysical core of his politics. Traditionalism is "more an attitude than an ism."
- **Dugin is neither original nor causal.** His ideas are not new and did not "cause" Russian aggression. He is more a symbol and an amplifier than an operational brain.
- **Convergence is not coordination.** There is no command center linking Moscow, Mar-a-Lago, and Silicon Valley. There is a **shared ideological ecosystem** (same enemies, same media, same deep sources) where the nodes recognize and cite one another. That is precisely what must be documented: not an org chart, an atmosphere.

The right formula for the dossier: Dugin is the **Russian and Traditionalist node** of an anti-liberal galaxy whose American tech-right is the **accelerationist node**. The two do not march in step, but they inhabit the same sky, and they know it.
