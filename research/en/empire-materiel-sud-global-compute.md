## 1. Karen Hao: AI as Empire

***Empire of AI: Dreams and Nightmares in Sam Altman's OpenAI*** (May 20, 2025): ~300 interviews, 7 years of reporting, 2025 National Book Critics Circle Award. The thesis articulates exactly what was missing from the dossier: the AI industry functions **like a colonial empire** (plundered data, exploited labor of the South, datacenters in water-stressed peripheries, monopolized knowledge), justified by a quasi-religious ideology of AGI ("AGI-pilled"). Hao had forged the "AI colonialism" framework as early as 2021 (MIT Tech Review series).

**Caveat on use**: in November 2025, Hao publicly acknowledged a conversion error (a factor of 1000×) on the water consumption of a Chilean datacenter in the published edition. This is the favorite line of attack of her detractors; cite the court figures rather than the book on this specific point.

The theoretical foundation: **Couldry & Mejias, *The Costs of Connection* (2019)** — "data colonialism" is not a metaphor but the continuation of colonial appropriation (the "4X" grid: Explore, Exploit, Expand, Exterminate). And **Kate Crawford, *Atlas of AI* (2021)** + the "Anatomy of an AI System" map (2018, with Vladan Joler), which traces every component of a voice assistant down to the mines. *(Nuance: the share attributable to AI in the extraction of Congolese cobalt, vs. batteries and smartphones, is rhetorically strong but empirically diffuse.)*

## 2. The Hidden Labor: Annotators and Moderators of the South

- **OpenAI/Sama, Kenya (2021-2022)**: 36 workers paid **$1.32 to $2/hour** to label descriptions of sexual abuse, hate, and violence meant to make ChatGPT "safe." AI safety, in the most concrete sense, was manufactured in Nairobi.
- **Meta/Sama lawsuit**: ~260 moderators at $2.20/h; on December 4, 2024, medical expert testimony entered into the record: **81% of moderators diagnosed with severe PTSD**. October 2024: the Kenyan Court of Appeal upholds the jurisdiction of local courts — a global precedent (the digital workspace as an anchor of jurisdiction).
- **May 2023: first African content moderators' union** (150+ Meta/ByteDance/OpenAI workers, with COWU Kenya). Figures: **Daniel Motaung** (fired for unionizing, the origin of the foundational lawsuit), **Mercy Mutemi** (lawyer for 180+ moderators: "digital colonialism").
- **Scale AI / Remotasks** (the company of Alexandr Wang, now head of Meta Superintelligence Labs — Part 18): collapse of rates in the Philippines (~$10/task → less than 1 cent, 2021), abrupt shutdown in Kenya/Nigeria/Pakistan (March 2024) leaving thousands unpaid; compliance with decent-work standards: 1 criterion out of 10 (Oxford Internet Institute, 2022). **Wang's biography is the passage through the looking glass: from the data sweatshop to the summit of AI power.**

## 3. Water and Land: Datacenters in Stress Zones

- **~2/3 of datacenters built since 2022 are in water-stress zones** (Bloomberg investigation, 160+ sites). ~57% of direct water comes from drinking-water networks.
- **Chile (Google Cerrillos)**: license revoked by an environmental court, projected consumption ≈ 5.3 billion liters/year, on the order of the residential consumption of the entire municipality (~88,000 inhabitants); Google forced to switch to air cooling. MOSACAT mobilization.
- **Uruguay (Google Canelones)**: project announced during the worst drought in 70 years; 7.6M liters of drinking water/day. Protesters' slogan: **"No es sequía, es saqueo"** ("it's not drought, it's plunder").
- **Mexico (Querétaro)**: 8 of the 12 regional aquifers already overexploited by the time the datacenters arrived.
- **June 2026**: Google formalizes water standards under pressure (2024: 7.2 billion gallons consumed, ~64% "replenished").

## 4. The Petromonarchies: Compute Diplomacy

Actors absent from the dossier yet central since 2025 — petrodollars converted into "trusted compute":

- **Stargate UAE** (May 2025, announced during Trump's visit to Abu Dhabi): G42 + OpenAI + Oracle + NVIDIA + Cisco + SoftBank; 1 GW cluster (up to $10B) in a "UAE-US AI Campus" aiming for 5 GW. Microsoft, in parallel (not in the consortium): $15.2B in commitments in the UAE, an export license for ~60,400 A100-equivalents.
- **HUMAIN** (May 2025, Saudi PIF, chaired by MBS): declared goal of "3rd global AI provider"; NVIDIA partnership raised to **600,000 GPUs over 3 years** (Nov. 2025); xAI the first customer of a 500 MW datacenter; AMD, Qualcomm, AWS; target of 6.6 GW over 10 years.
- **Concept: "electrons into influence"** — the Gulf monarchies convert energy abundance into geopolitical leverage, under American export control ("third-party diffusion," §8). The dossier's techno-fascism now has openly non-democratic branch offices, without even the liberal veneer.

## 5. India: The "Third Way" of DPI

- **India AI Impact Summit, New Delhi, February 16-20, 2026**: the first global AI summit organized by a country of the South (118 delegations, 20+ heads of state), pillars "People, Planet, Progress." It is also the summit at which Washington declares: "We totally reject global governance of AI" (Part 17).
- **IndiaAI Mission** (March 2024, ~$1.7B); the "Democratising AI Resources" working group is co-chaired by India, Egypt, and Kenya.
- India positions its **DPI** (Digital Public Infrastructure: Aadhaar, UPI, DigiLocker, 1.4B citizens) as a blueprint of "sovereign AI" exportable to the South — a "third way" between the US and China. **A documented double edge**: real public infrastructure AND a mass biometric identification machine, capturable by the security state as by the private sector.

## 6. The Silicon Layer: NVIDIA, TSMC, ASML

The most concentrated power in the entire dossier is neither in Washington nor in the models, it is in three companies:

- **NVIDIA: the world's largest market cap, $5,296B as of June 4, 2026** (first to cross $5,000B, October 2025; peak >$5,500B in May 2026). **Jensen Huang** publicly targets $10,000B and commits ~$150B/year in spending in Taiwan. Politically: he called export controls a "**failure**" (Computex, May 2025: NVIDIA's market share in China fell from ~95% to ~50%) and hailed Trump's reversal ("a great reversal of a wrong policy"). Huang theorizes nothing; he controls the physical bottleneck of all global AI and shuttles between the White House and Beijing. **He is the No. 1 power figure absent from Part 8.**
- **TSMC**: ~90% of advanced chip production. The "**silicon shield**" doctrine: TSMC's centrality would deter an invasion of Taiwan (critical version: it makes it a target). A $1B fine for ~2.9M dies that went to Huawei via Sophgo.
- **ASML** (Netherlands): absolute global monopoly on EUV machines. According to Bloomberg (May 2024, not officially confirmed), ASML and TSMC have a remote "**kill switch**" on EUV machines in case of a Chinese invasion. A European chokepoint made invisible by US-centric narratives — and now lead investor in Mistral (Part 18).
- **The 2025-26 export controls saga, summarized**: Biden's AI Diffusion Rule (Jan. 2025, 3 country tiers) repealed by Trump (May 13, 2025); H20 deal (Aug. 2025): 15% of NVIDIA's Chinese revenue paid back to the US government; Truth Social announcement (December 8, 2025) to authorize the H200 (~6× the H20) against a 25% levy — **a proposal apparently abandoned in the final BIS rule of January 2026** (cap of ~50% of US volume, with no levy). The same day as the H200 announcement, the DOJ dismantled "Operation Gatekeeper" (≥$160M of smuggled GPUs to China); in March 2026, the arrest of a Super Micro co-founder (a $2.5B export conspiracy). **The Chip Security Act** (House vote approved March 2026) would impose physical location trackers on chips.
- **Huawei Ascend**: ~800,000 units produced in 2025, target of 1.6M dies in 2026, ~80% of the performance of an H100, real bottleneck on HBM memory. *(SemiAnalysis/Bloomberg figures, medium confidence.)*

## 7. Energy: Resurrected Nuclear, Unpermitted Gas

- **Datacenters ≈ 1,050 TWh projected in 2026** (the world's 5th-largest consuming "country"). USA: from 176 TWh (2023, 4.4% of electricity) toward 325-580 TWh (6.7-12%) in 2028.
- **Three Mile Island restarts for Microsoft**: Unit 1 (renamed Crane Clean Energy Center, 835 MW), a 20-year PPA (~$1.6B), target 2027, a federal loan guarantee of up to $1B. The first restart of a closed nuclear plant in American history. The symbolism is immense: the founding accident of American nuclear fear resurrected to train models.
- **xAI Colossus (Memphis/Southaven)**: 33 **unpermitted** gas turbines (potential 2,508 t/year of NOx, in a zone already rated "F" for ozone, with a majority-Black population). **The NAACP sues xAI on April 14, 2026** (Clean Air Act); the EPA closed in January 2026 the regulatory loophole that was exploited. Figure: Abre' Conner (NAACP): asking "Black and frontline communities to bear the toxic burden of 'innovation.'" **Environmental justice enters the dossier as a real counter-power (to be added to Part 10).**
- **Stargate US** (OpenAI/SoftBank/Oracle/MGX, "up to $500B"): >$100B deployed (Jan. 2026), 8 sites, ~7 GW planned, but a freeze on expansions over financing disagreements (March 2026). Bottleneck No. 1: electricity (PPAs with 3-5 years of lead time).

## 8. Concepts: Governing Through Computation

- **Compute governance** (Lennart Heim, RAND; arXiv:2402.08797): governing AI by governing its physical resource, because compute is the only input that is *detectable, excludable, quantifiable, with an ultra-concentrated supply chain*. It is the theoretical foundation of both the Yudkowsky-Soares program (Part 14) and of export controls.
- **Compute thresholds**: FLOP thresholds as legal triggers (10²⁶ under Biden, repealed; 10²⁵ in the AI Act); announced obsolescence with test-time compute.
- **Hardware-enabled governance**: inscribing the rule into the silicon (trackers, kill switches, attestation). The Chip Security Act is the first legislative translation of this. A shift of law toward matter.
- **"Governing through the cloud"** (Heim): making the hyperscalers the gatekeepers of regulation — sovereignty delegated to the same oligopolies the dossier documents.
- **Tiered AI diffusion** (RAND): the world carved into chip-access tiers; the United States as the "long-term power broker" of global infrastructure. **Sovereignty of computation as formalized technological imperialism.**

## 9. Verdict

1. **AI power has three layers: ideas, capital, matter.** The dossier documented the first two; the third is the most concentrated of all (one company for GPUs, one for etching, one for the etching machines) and the least democratically governed.
2. **Extraction is not collateral damage, it is the model**: Kenyan wages at $1.32/h, Latin American aquifers, Memphis NOx. The AI value chain reproduces the classic colonial geography: extraction in the South, value in the North, externalities in both.
3. **The new entrants are not democracies**: petromonarchies converted into "trusted" compute hubs, biometric India as a "third way." The question of Part 11 (concentration vs. democratic control) now poses itself at a planetary scale, and the empirical answer of 2026 is everywhere the same.
4. **For the techno-fascism grid (Part 13)**: the material layer provides what the ideological grids cannot see — the infrastructure of a power that no longer needs to convince, only to own the bottlenecks.
