# The Third Ways: Escaping the e/acc vs Doomers Standoff

> Documented research report. Written June 5, 2026. Sources at the end of the document.
> Part 19: the dossier opposed two camps (accelerationist techno-optimists / longtermist doomers). But the real debate of 2024-2026 is crossed by **third ways** that this binary erases — and one of them, Dan McQuillan's, literally carries the dossier's keyword in its title.
> Thesis: there is not ONE third way, but at least five competing and often incompatible poles. Mapping them restores the dossier's depth of field.
> Key claims verified by adversarial counter-research.

## 1. Why the e/acc vs doom binary is a trap

The dossier (Part 5) structures the field along the "accelerate ↔ secure" axis. But that axis is itself a construction of the two camps that benefit from it: it **presupposes that the only question is speed** (fast or slow), evacuating the questions of *who decides*, *for whom*, and *which technology*. Yet the most interesting thinkers of 2024-2026 reject precisely that axis. Part 11 had formulated it for the caution camp (the real cleavage is concentration vs democracy); here we generalize it: **there are at least five third ways, mutually incompatible, that shift the axis of the debate.**

## 2. Dan McQuillan: the anti-fascist approach (the abolitionist pole)

The dossier's most direct reference, and yet absent until now.

- ***Resisting AI: An Anti-fascist Approach to Artificial Intelligence*** (Bristol University Press, 2022). McQuillan (Goldsmiths/Essex). **Precise thesis — not to be distorted**: AI is NOT intrinsically fascist; it lends itself to a **fascization**. "The social contradictions amplified by AI are the ones fascism will claim to resolve." AI provides the **bureaucratic infrastructure of discrimination** (ranking, scoring, exclusion) that fascism mobilizes. This is exactly the theoretical bridge Part 13 (anatomy of the concept) was looking for.
- **"Decomputing"** (a concept developed 2024-2025): an active refusal of AI expansion, combining degrowth, decoloniality and conviviality. McQuillan calls himself an "**AI abolitionist**" and ChatGPT a "bullshit engine." Seminar (Essex, October 2024): "An anti-fascist approach to AI means decomputing." Follow-up: "Resisting GenAI & Big Tech in Higher Education" (November 2025).
- **Position**: neither accelerate nor "secure" (which he sees as legitimation), but **abolish/degrow**. The pole that the binary renders literally unthinkable.

## 3. Vitalik Buterin: d/acc (the crypto-defensive pole)

The third way from inside tech.

- **"My techno-optimism"** (November 27, 2023) introduces **d/acc** as an explicit response to the OpenAI/EA vs e/acc schism. The "d": **defense, democracy, decentralization, differential** technological development (a concept borrowed from Bostrom).
- **"d/acc: one year later"** (January 5, 2025): the central model opposes a "**defense-dominant**" world to an "**offense-dominant**" world ("the darkest moments in history come when offense has a clear advantage"). Two proposed mechanisms: graduated legal liability for AI deployers, and a worldwide hardware "**soft pause**" capable of reducing compute by 90-99% in an emergency. *(A speculative proposal, with no documented traction.)*
- **Position**: **selectively** accelerate defensive and power-distributing technologies, not offensive and centralizing ones. Replaces the "fast/slow" axis with "which technology shifts the attack/defense balance." Comes from the crypto milieu (Part 4) but orthogonal to Andreessen.

## 4. Left accelerationism (the Marxist pole)

The inverted political mirror of e/acc, which the dossier had ignored.

- **Srnicek & Williams, "Accelerate: Manifesto for an Accelerationist Politics"** (2013), then ***Inventing the Future*** (2015); **Aaron Bastani, *Fully Automated Luxury Communism*** (2019). The L/acc thesis: develop AI within a democratic framework, toward just ends (automate work + universal income + collective ownership of the means of automation).
- What it shares with e/acc: the techno-optimist ground (technology can liberate). The opposition: who owns, who decides. **Nick Land himself ruled** (Guardian, 2017): "the notion that self-propelling technology is separable from capitalism is a deep theoretical error" — the founder of right accelerationism denying all legitimacy to its left version.
- **Position**: accelerate + democracy + collective ownership. Shows that "techno-optimism" does not belong to the right: Hinton (Part 18, "socialism") is an involuntary cousin.

## 5. Yuk Hui: cosmotechnics (the decolonial-philosophical pole)

The only properly philosophical alternative (already introduced in Part 15).

- Two books in 2024: ***Machine and Sovereignty: For a Planetary Thinking*** and ***Post-Europe***. Lecture "In Defence of Technodiversity" (Glasgow, March 13, 2025): he **explicitly criticizes both dominant attitudes — acceleration AND deceleration — as the same dead end**.
- **Cosmotechnics**: there is no ONE universal technology; each cosmology generates its own relation to technics. **Technodiversity**: against the technological monism (the idea of a single path, a single future) shared by Silicon Valley AND by China's military-civil fusion (Part 15).
- **Position**: exit monism. Neither speed nor stoppage, but a **plurality of technical futures**. The pole that reframes the entire debate as a false Western universal.

## 6. "AI as normal technology" (the anti-hype pole)

The most influential "neither doom nor hype" critique on the academic side.

- **Narayanan & Kapoor (Princeton), "AI as Normal Technology"** (April 15, 2025, Knight First Amendment Institute, 15,000+ words): treat AI like electricity or the Internet — transformative but "normal," under human control and likely to remain so. **The pace of change is set by adoption, not by innovation.** Aim for resilience rather than existential fear. Defuses the utopia (Amodei) and the apocalypse (Yudkowsky) simultaneously. Follow-up to ***AI Snake Oil*** (2024). Tool-concept: the "**capability-reliability gap**" (the gap between benchmark performance and real work).
- **Emily Bender & Alex Hanna, *The AI Con*** (May 13, 2025): heirs of "Stochastic Parrots" (2021); hype as an **operation of power** (inflating capabilities to justify investment and deregulation).
- **Meredith Whittaker** (president of Signal, ex-Google, organizer of the 2018 walkout): a technical critique of **agentic AI** as a threat to privacy (SXSW March 2025: "putting your brain in a jar"; Fortune Nov. 2025: an "existential threat" to any app via prompt injection, OS-level integration being a "very dangerous architectural decision"). The critique comes from inside the privacy industry.
- **Timnit Gebru & Émile Torres**: the **TESCREAL** paper (First Monday, April 1, 2024) — already in Part 4, but the framework goes further: the ideological bundle (transhumanism → longtermism) is rooted in Anglo-American eugenics.
- **Position**: demystify. Neither doom nor hype: AI is a banal object of power, to be regulated as such.

## 7. Public AI and the commons (the social-democratic pole)

The concrete institutional alternative.

- **Public AI Network**: ~350 members by late 2025, 100+ organizations (Mozilla, Internet Archive, Open Future, Berkman Klein). The idea: an AI **democratically funded, provided and governed** by public bodies — a "public option" on the model of a national health service.
- **CalCompute** (California): public compute infrastructure governed by a consortium of academics, unions and public-interest advocates. *(Law passed, number/date to be confirmed — possibly SB 53.)*
- **European "AI factories"**: €750M for 7 sites (Dec. 2024), 6 more in October 2025. *(Figures from secondary sources.)*
- Theoretical bedrock: **digital commons, data cooperatives, data trusts** — collective governance (Ostrom, Nobel 2009, updated for the digital age) against enclosure by Big Tech.
- **Position**: neither private nor authoritarian-statist, but **public-democratic**. The institutional content that Part 10 (counter-powers) was missing.

## 8. Morozov: socialism after AI

The left third way that criticizes the other left ways.

- **Evgeny Morozov, "Socialism After AI"** (The Ideas Letter, December 11, 2025): the left treats AI as a neutral, redirectable tool, whereas it is a **force that shapes values**. Socialism must become a project of "**worldmaking**" (the production of new cultural worlds), not of regulation/planning (taxing, nationalizing). His podcast **"The Santiago Boys"** (2023) opposes a "Santiago School of technology" (Allende's Cybersyn) to the "Chicago School."
- **A documented internal fracture**: Aaron Benanav and Leif Weatherby reply to him in the same issue (Benanav defends a "multi-criterial" economy). **The left third way is itself fractured** — proof that there is no single "third camp."

## 9. Verdict: five poles, not one camp

| Pole | Figure(s) | Slogan | Relation to the speed axis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Abolitionist** | McQuillan | "Decomputing" | Exits the axis: degrow |
| **Crypto-defensive** | Buterin (d/acc) | Defense over offense | Accelerate selectively |
| **Marxist/L-acc** | Srnicek, Bastani | Automated luxury + UBI | Accelerate + collective ownership |
| **Decolonial** | Yuk Hui | Technodiversity | Pluralize: neither fast nor slow |
| **Anti-hype** | Narayanan, Bender, Whittaker, Gebru | "Normal technology" | The speed axis is a hype illusion |
| **Social-democratic** | Public AI, Morozov | Public AI / worldmaking | Democratize the decision |

**Three conclusions:**
1. **The e/acc vs doom binary is itself ideological.** It serves the two incumbent camps (Parts 1 and 11) by reducing the debate to speed. The five poles above contest it from five different directions — and their only common point is to shift the question from *fast/slow* to *who/for whom/which technology*.
2. **McQuillan is the missing piece of Part 13**: he supplies the articulated theory of "fascization through AI" (AI as the infrastructure of discrimination that fascism mobilizes), with a precision that the concept of "techno-fascism" (Coeckelbergh) lacked. To be read before any working definition.
3. **These ways are incompatible with one another** (libertarian-crypto d/acc vs degrowth decomputing vs Marxist L-acc vs decolonial cosmotechnics vs social-democratic Public AI). Presenting them as "a third camp" would reproduce the binary's error of simplification. The Morozov/Benanav debate (Dec. 2025) proves it: even the left does not speak with one voice. **It is this plurality — and not a single alternative — that is the real exit from the standoff.**
