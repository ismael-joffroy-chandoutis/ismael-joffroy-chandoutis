## 1. Three Claims Not to Be Confused

The trap of a eugenics part is the conflation. Three different claims circulate, and crushing them together produces a false and attackable accusation:

1. **The rationalist/EA community has a documented overlap with the "human biodiversity" (HBD) milieus and scientific racism.** True, but localized to a precise infrastructure.
2. **Such-and-such individual is a eugenicist.** True for some, false for others. To be established name by name.
3. **The logic of longtermism recycles a eugenic reasoning.** This is the thesis of Gebru and Torres. A structural critique, not a neutral fact.

Held separately, these three claims form a solid genealogy. Conflated, they give "the rationalists are eugenicists," which is false. This entire part consists of holding them apart.

## 2. The Named Intellectual Carrier: Bostrom

Nick Bostrom, founder of longtermism and author of *Superintelligence* (Part 11), has a record that is not inferred but documented. Two pieces:

- A **racist email from 1996** (in which he used the n-word, claiming to believe Black people on average less intelligent), for which he apologized in **January 2023** when the message resurfaced.
- More structurally: his founding text **"Existential Risks" (2002)** listed **"dysgenic pressures"** among the existential risks, that is, the fear of a decline in the genetic "quality" of future intelligence. This is not a private slip, it is inscribed in the canon of existential risk.

Bostrom is therefore the point where the eugenic seam enters directly into the x-risk doctrine. With him, the link is explicit.

## 3. The Infrastructure That Platforms It: Manifest and Lighthaven

The second site of the seam is not an idea, it is a building. **Manifest** is a conference on prediction markets held since 2023 at the **Lighthaven Campus** in Berkeley, run by **Lightcone Infrastructure**, a project descended from CFAR and the parent of LessWrong. It is the rationalist infrastructure itself.

The 2023 and 2024 editions invited HBD figures:
- **Jonathan Anomaly**, author of an article titled "Defending Eugenics," introduced at Manifest 2023 as "professor and eugenicist," who advocates polygenic embryo selection (IQ, disease resistance) guided by prediction markets.
- **Stephen Hsu**, former vice-president of Michigan State (resigned in 2020 after controversy), a specialist in genomics and intelligence. A Yale professor (Daniel HoSang, Anti-Eugenics Collective) describes him as "a bridge between explicit racists like Ron Unz, Steve Sailer, Stefan Molyneux, and mainstream figures in tech and science."
- **Richard Hanania** (2023), whose pseudonymous white-nationalist writings from the early 2010s were documented by HuffPost.

A *Guardian* investigation (June 2024) linked FTX/Sam Bankman-Fried money (~$5M) to the purchase of the venue, and accused Manifest of "platforming people linked to scientific racism."

**The denials must appear, for fairness and because they are themselves informative.** Organizer Austin Chen: "We did not invite them to give talks about race and IQ"; "Manifest has no specific views on eugenics or race & IQ." Lightcone's director, Oliver Habryka, disputes the use of FTX funds ("No FTX funds were used in the purchase of Lighthaven") and invokes an open-rental policy (the venue rents to whoever pays, without endorsement). *Quillette* accuses the Guardian of having lumped together technologists, EAs, and eugenicists. And an attendee's report, on the EA Forum itself, honestly distinguishes "genuinely curious academics whose subject will get them into trouble (like Hsu)" from those who are "edgy for edge's sake (like Hanania)."

The just conclusion is therefore not "Manifest is a eugenicist conference." It is: **the rationalist infrastructure has an open-door policy that regularly platforms the HBD seam, and a public curious about these subjects gathers there.** It is not a doctrine, it is a porosity. But a repeated porosity is not an accident.

## 4. The Structural Thesis: TESCREAL and Eugenic Logic

The third level is that of **Timnit Gebru and Émile P. Torres** and their acronym **TESCREAL** (Part 12). Their thesis: the "bundle" of Silicon Valley ideologies (transhumanism, extropianism, singularitarianism, cosmism, rationalism, effective altruism, longtermism) has **eugenic roots** and its logic recycles them. Maximizing the value of future humanity, worrying about the "quality" of intelligence to come, subordinating the present to 10^58 future lives: this is eugenic reasoning reformulated in probabilistic language.

A capital clarification, which avoids the conflation: this critique targets the **longtermist / x-risk wing**, not the EA of anti-malaria bednets. "Global health" effective altruism (GiveWell, deworming, animal welfare) is rather humanitarian and center-left. The eugenic seam is in the wing that thinks the distant future and the "trajectory" of the species, not in the one that counts the lives saved today.

## 5. Those Who Are Not Part of the Seam

This is the section that makes the accusation credible, because it refuses contamination by contiguity:

- **Eliezer Yudkowsky** was present at Manifest. He is not for all that a carrier of HBD: no trace of racialist statements from him. To place him as a eugenicist would be an error. He is in the rationalist **pot** (he founded it), not a carrier of **that ingredient**. He is an out-of-tribe doomer (Part 18).
- **Yoshua Bengio** is not at all in this subculture. Mainstream academia, a social-democratic register, zero link with the seam (Part 18). To rank him there would be absurd.
- **Charitable EA** (global health) must be distinguished from longtermist EA. Same word, two worlds.

A part that put everyone in the same bag would do the opposite of its purpose: it would discredit the entire dossier. The strength of the accusation rests on its delimitation.

## 6. Eugenics 2.0: Embryo Selection, the Hinge with the Gender Part

The seam does not stay theoretical. It takes flesh in a practice, and that is where it meets Part 29 (gender, pronatalism). **Simone and Malcolm Collins**, public faces of tech pronatalism, practice **preimplantation embryo selection by polygenic scores** (via Genomic Prediction; an undercover reporter was shown a ranking of their embryos on an IQ curve, company PolygenX). They describe themselves as "hipster eugenicists" while rejecting the term "eugenics" on a definitional basis: for them, eugenics presupposes **state coercion**, so a voluntary parental choice would not be eugenics.

This is precisely the slide to document. Antonio Regalado (MIT Technology Review) calls it "**Eugenics 2.0**." Historical eugenics was state-run and coercive (sterilizations, racial laws). Its 2026 version is private, voluntary, market-based, sold as "giving your children the best start." The coercion has disappeared, the genetic sorting remains. Pronatalism (have more children) plus selection (make the "good" children) closes the loop.

## 7. Verdict: Why This Part Is Load-Bearing

1. **It is the link that turns the metaphor into a lineage.** Historical fascism was not only authoritarian, it was racial-hygienist: American eugenics inspired the Nazi laws, "race" was the heart of the project. Without this part, "techno-fascism" stays an atmospheric comparison. With it, there is a documented continuity, from Bostrom to Anomaly to the IQ curve of the Collins embryos.
2. **The seam is real but concentrated.** It runs through Bostrom (the x-risk canon), through the Lighthaven infrastructure (the open door), through longtermist logic (Gebru/Torres), and through embryo selection (the Collins). It does not run everywhere, and saying who is exempt from it (Yudkowsky, Bengio, charitable EA) is what makes the diagnosis serious.
3. **Denial is, once again, the mechanism.** "No views on eugenics" (Manifest), "it isn't eugenics without state coercion" (Collins). As in Part 24 and Part 26: the system works all the better because it can say "that isn't it." The dossier holds both: cite the denials, AND show the practice.
