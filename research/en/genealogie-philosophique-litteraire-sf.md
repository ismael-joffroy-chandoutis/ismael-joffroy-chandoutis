## 1. Introduction: Reading Tech Through Its Libraries

A distinctive feature of the *tech-right* is that it **cites its sources**. The manifestos (Andreessen), the books (Thiel's *Zero to One*, Srinivasan's *The Network State*) and the company names form a **coherent library**. Deciphering it makes it possible to understand that what presents itself as "disruptive innovation" is rooted in an **old genealogy**: Austrian economics, Nietzscheanism, Randian objectivism, Italian futurism, neoreaction, and a very precise **science-fiction** imaginary.

---

## 2. The Philosophical Sources

### a) Liberal/Libertarian Economics
- **Adam Smith, David Ricardo, Frédéric Bastiat**: the market, the division of labor, "what is seen and what is not seen."
- **The Austrian School, Ludwig von Mises, Friedrich Hayek, Israel Kirzner**: the market as a **"discovery machine"** (Andreessen's phrase comes from Hayek), spontaneous order, distrust of planning. Hayek is central: he **substitutes market success for birth** as the criterion of social superiority.
- **Milton & David Friedman, Thomas Sowell, George Gilder, Deirdre McCloskey, Julian Simon**: monetarism, anti-Malthusianism (Simon: "the human mind is the ultimate resource," hence Andreessen's "50 billion inhabitants").

### b) Nietzscheanism and "Aristocratic Radicalism"
- **Friedrich Nietzsche**: the overman, the will to power, the idea that **the purpose of culture is the production of great men**, not equality. This is the matrix of Andreessen's *"Becoming Technological Supermen."*
- **Vilfredo Pareto, James Burnham, Robert Michels**: theories of **elites** and of the "iron law of oligarchy" (every organization ends up dominated by a minority).

### c) Objectivism
- **Ayn Rand** (and her character **John Galt**, the "patron saint" of the manifesto), **Isabel Paterson, Rose Wilder Lane**: rational egoism, the heroic creator against the parasitic masses, the state as predator.

### d) Italian Futurism
- **Filippo Tommaso Marinetti**: the *Manifesto of Futurism* (1909), the cult of speed, of the machine, of creative violence. Andreessen **paraphrases it directly**. The problem: Marinetti **co-signed the Fascist Manifesto (1919)**.

### e) Mimetic Theory and Political Theology (the Thiel pole)
- **René Girard** (mimetic desire, the scapegoat), **Leo Strauss** (esoteric reading), **Carl Schmitt** (friend/enemy, the state of exception).

### f) Neoreaction (the Dark Enlightenment)
- **Curtis Yarvin (Mencius Moldbug)** and **Nick Land**: democracy as a failing system, to be replaced by an **authoritarian/monarchical governance** of the "corporate state" type. Land links accelerationism to **capitalism as an inhuman intelligence** ("democracy tends to fascism," reversed into praise).

### g) Transhumanism/Longtermism (the TESCREAL pole)
- **Max More** (extropianism), **Nick Bostrom** (existential risk, superintelligence), **Ray Kurzweil** (the Singularity ~2045), **Eliezer Yudkowsky** (rationalism), **William MacAskill / Toby Ord** (longtermism). *(Detailed in the "Transhumanism & Crypto" part.)*

## 3. The Literary and Science-Fiction Sources

SF is not a backdrop: it is a **reservoir of scenarios** that the founders seek to realize.

### *The Sovereign Individual* (1997) — the key text
By **James Dale Davidson & William Rees-Mogg** (father of Jacob Rees-Mogg). Not fiction, but a prophetic essay: technology will enable **powerful individuals (financiers, creators, entrepreneurs) to "exit" the nation-state**, giving rise to a new "sovereign" aristocracy. **A cult book for Thiel, Srinivasan and, in part, Andreessen.** It is the intellectual matrix of "exit," of crypto and of the "network state."

### *Snow Crash* (1992) — Neal Stephenson
Invents the word **"metaverse"** (which would inspire Facebook's pivot to Meta) and the **"avatar."** A **dystopian** novel: an ultra-privatized future in which the state has shattered into corporate franchises and enclaves. Read by the Valley as a **roadmap** rather than a warning. *The Diamond Age* (nanotech, AI-based education) is the other cult Stephenson.

### *The Culture* — Iain M. Banks
An SF cycle describing a **post-scarcity, anarchist and abundant utopia**, run by benevolent AIs (the "Minds"). **Elon Musk** cites it as "the best vision of the AI future"; **SpaceX names its recovery barges** after Banks's sentient ships (*Just Read the Instructions*, *Of Course I Still Love You*, *A Shortfall of Gravitas*). **Jeff Bezos** is a fan (Amazon's adaptation of *Consider Phlebas*). The irony: Banks was a **socialist** and the Culture is the antithesis of capitalism, a major misreading.

### Other Recurring References
- **Isaac Asimov, *Foundation***: "psychohistory," the idea of **saving/guiding civilization** through a scientific elite. Very frequently cited by Musk.
- **Frank Herbert, *Dune***: power, ecology, eugenics (the Kwisatz Haderach), often **misread** (Herbert intended a *warning* against charismatic saviors).
- **Robert Heinlein, *The Moon Is a Harsh Mistress***: a libertarian lunar colony ("TANSTAAFL").
- **William Gibson, *Neuromancer***: cyberpunk, "cyberspace," corporations against the state.
- **J.R.R. Tolkien**: see section 4.
- **Vernor Vinge**: invents the technical concept of the **Singularity** (1993).
- **Charles Stross, *Accelerando***: technological acceleration and posthumanity (Stross, like Banks, is highly **critical** of the "tech-bro" appropriation).

## 4. The Case of Company Names (Tolkien & Co.)

The Thiel orbit has turned *The Lord of the Rings* into a **company nomenclature**:
- **Palantir**, the *palantíri*, the seeing-stones (surveillance/data).
- **Anduril** (Palmer Luckey, funded by Founders Fund), Aragorn's sword, the "Flame of the West" (drones/defense).
- **Mithril Capital** (Thiel), the precious elvish metal.
- **Erebor** (a banking project), the Lonely Mountain of *The Hobbit*.
- **Lonsdale / Palantir** and other nods.

Critics (Crikey, *Disconnect*, *The Informed Alarmist*) underline the **irony**: in Tolkien, the palantír **corrupts** its user (Denethor, Saruman), the Ring of Power must be **destroyed**, and industrialization (Saruman, the scouring of the Shire) is **evil**. The founders identify with an imaginary whose morality they invert.

## 5. The "Misreading": They Read Dystopia as an Instruction Manual

This is the recurring thread (underlined by *Slate*, 2026; Paul Waldman; Damien Walter): **the tech-right confuses warnings with programs**.
- *Snow Crash* (the collapse of the state) → a model, not a deterrent.
- *The Culture* (a **socialist** utopia) → claimed by **capitalists**.
- *Dune* / *Foundation* (critiques of the **savior** and of determinism) → read as praise for the genius guiding the masses.
- Tolkien (a critique of **power** and of industrialization) → branding for surveillance and weaponry.

This **systematic misreading** is not trivial: it reveals a **magical thinking** in which fiction validates ambition, stripped of its critical charge. SF supplies the **narrative grandeur** ("slaying dragons, bringing home the loot," writes Andreessen) without the **moral prudence** that these works carried.

| Family | Key Figures | Idea Imported into Tech |
|---|---|---|
| Austrian econ. | Hayek, Mises, Kirzner | market = "discovery machine," anti-regulation |
| Anti-Malthusianism | Julian Simon | "the human is the ultimate resource" → pro-natalism |

| Objectivism | Ayn Rand (John Galt) | heroic creator vs parasitic masses |
| Futurism | Marinetti | cult of machine/speed/violence (proto-fascism) |

| Neoreaction | Yarvin, Nick Land | anti-democracy, corporate state/monarchy |
| Transhumanism | More, Bostrom, Kurzweil | Singularity, surpassing the human |
| "Exit" essay | *The Sovereign Individual* | exiting the nation-state, individual sovereignty |
| Dystopian SF | Stephenson (*Snow Crash*) | metaverse, post-state franchises |
| Utopian SF | Iain M. Banks (*Culture*) | benevolent AIs, post-scarcity (misread) |
| "Elite-guides" SF | Asimov (*Foundation*), Herbert (*Dune*) | psychohistory, the savior (misread) |
| Cyberpunk | Gibson (*Neuromancer*) | cyberspace, corporations > state |
| Fantasy | Tolkien | Palantir/Anduril/Mithril nomenclature |
