## 1. Technocracy vs Democracy

The central cleavage of the entire dossier. It is not about the speed of technology, but about who decides, and on what basis.

| | Democracy | Technocracy |
|---|---|---|
| Who decides? | The many, or accountable and revocable institutions (parliaments, states, UN) | A small elite of "the knowing" (experts, engineers, initiates) |
| Legitimacy drawn from… | The popular mandate, the vote, public debate | Expertise, intelligence, mastery of the subject |
| View of the public | Sovereign, even if fallible | Too ignorant or irrational to decide |
| Method | Deliberation, transparency, compromise, counter-powers | Expert decision, often under emergency |
| Motto | "No one has the right to decide in the people's place" | "It's too complex or important to leave to a vote" |

Why this structures the series: e/acc (Andreessen), neo-reaction (Yarvin), longtermism (Bostrom), and doomerism (Yudkowsky) clash violently over the speed of AI, but often share the same technocratic reflex, the idea that an enlightened elite must decide the fate of humanity. Their common point is a distrust of mass democracy.

> Trap to avoid: "anti-accelerationist" does not equal "democrat." One can want to slow down AI for elitist reasons ("only the wise should control AGI"), which is just as anti-democratic as acceleration. That is why Bengio (public, multilateral governance) is a genuine counter-power, where Yudkowsky ("stop everything" by expert decision) remains technocratic. See [Part 11](./camp-prudence-bengio-bostrom-yudkowsky.md).

## 2. Accelerationism and e/acc

**Definition.** Accelerationism is the idea that the technological and capitalist process must be intensified rather than slowed, to reach a rupture. **e/acc** (effective accelerationism) is its 2020s Silicon Valley version, popularized by Andreessen and a swarm of anonymous accounts: technological progress is a moral good in itself, any "deceleration" is a betrayal, energy and intelligence must grow without limit.

**Roots.** "Left" accelerationism (Srnicek, Williams) and above all the "dark" accelerationism of Nick Land (Part 7), where AI-capital is an intelligence that deploys itself through the human and surpasses it. Marinetti and futurism are its aesthetic ancestor (Part 6).

**Verdict, bullshit or not?** *A real intuition, an interested fatalism.*
- Defensible: technology really does transform the world, and pure refusal is rarely a politics.
- Bullshit: turning a fact (it accelerates) into a duty (we must accelerate) is a sophism, and it serves those who profit from acceleration. "You can't stop the bus" (Amodei) disguises a choice as a fatality. See [Part 1](./manifeste-techno-optimiste-andreessen.md).

## 3. Transhumanism

**Definition.** A movement that wants to use technology to overcome human biological limits (aging, death, disease, cognitive limits), to the point of creating an augmented or even post-human being.

**What it concretely covers:** radical life extension, brain implants (Neuralink type), uploading of consciousness, genetic editing, cryonics (Yudkowsky is signed up). Roots: extropianism (Max More, 1988-92), Kurzweil and the Singularity (~2045). It is the T of TESCREAL.

**Verdict, bullshit or not?** *A serious core plus a religious drift.*
- Defensible: to cure, extend life, augment capacities, medicine already does this. A legitimate philosophical question.
- Bullshit: digital immortality, uploading, cryonics are unproven, quasi-religious promises ("the Geek Paradise"), and an elite fantasy (who will have access to augmentation? the rich). Risk: a two-tier medicine erected into a civilizational project.

## 4. Rationalism (in the Yudkowsky/LessWrong sense)

**Caution:** nothing to do with classical philosophical rationalism (Descartes, Spinoza). Here, it is a subcultural movement born from the LessWrong blog (Yudkowsky, 2009).

**Definition.** The conviction that one can "debug" human thought to decide more justly, via Bayesian probability, the hunt for cognitive biases, and reasoning "from first principles." It is the R of TESCREAL.

**Verdict, bullshit or not?** *Good tools plus a dangerous arrogance.*
- Defensible: knowing your biases, thinking in probabilities, demanding evidence is genuinely useful.
- Bullshit: the arrogance of believing oneself "the rational one" and judging "irrational" anyone who diverges. It produces delusional quantified certainties (P(doom) = 100%) that resemble rigor but rest on bogus assumptions. Plus a sectarian culture (jargon, gurus), out of which, as a side effect, neo-reaction emerged (Yarvin), and where the HBD seam gathers (Part 28).

## 5. TESCREAL, the Ideological "Bundle"

A critical acronym coined by Timnit Gebru and Émile P. Torres to designate a "bundle" of interwoven Silicon Valley ideologies:

**T**ranshumanism, **E**xtropianism, **S**ingularitarianism, **C**osmism, **R**ationalism, **E**ffective Altruism, **L**ongtermism.

**The thesis:** these seven currents form a single whole with shared roots (including a eugenic genealogy, Part 28), a single techno-utopian faith, and a single tendency to subordinate the present to a grandiose future (galactic colonization, 10^58 future lives). Hence elitist and authoritarian implications, even when the discourse claims to be humanist.

> TESCREAL is the decoding tool that links the parts together: transhumanism and crypto ([Part 4](./transhumanisme-crypto-convergences.md)), the caution camp ([Part 11](./camp-prudence-bengio-bostrom-yudkowsky.md)), counter-powers ([Part 10](./contre-pouvoirs.md)), eugenics ([Part 28](./eugenisme-tescreal-filiation.md)).

## 6. Concepts Added (Parts 13 to 29)

**Vectofascism** (Chatonsky, [Part 24](./vectofascisme-chatonsky-wark.md)). A contemporary form of fascism adapted to the AI era: no longer the fascists who use technology, but the mathematical properties of vector spaces that reconfigure the political. Personalized enemies for each node, the "industrialized differend" (impossibility of agreeing on the very terms of the debate), affective modulation instead of mass mobilization, spectrality (a power that can always say "I am not here"). *Verdict: a powerful concept, to be handled with a safeguard, its very unfalsifiability.*

**Technofeudalism** (Varoufakis, [Part 10](./contre-pouvoirs.md)). The thesis that capitalism has mutated into a new feudalism: the platforms are no longer markets but fiefs (cloud, app stores) that extract a rent and from which one can no longer exit. Explains why there is "no clean outside" to the critique (Part 16 and the authorship note). *Verdict: a useful description, debated as a theoretical rupture.*

**Compute governance** (Heim/RAND, [Part 16](./empire-materiel-sud-global-compute.md)). Governing AI by governing its physical resource, computation, because compute is the only input that is detectable, excludable, quantifiable, and with an ultra-concentrated supply chain. The theoretical foundation of both export controls and Yudkowsky's moratorium. Declines into FLOP thresholds, registries, and "hardware-enabled governance" (inscribing the rule in the silicon). *Verdict: a real lever, dual-use (democratic regulation or imperialism of computation).*

**Circular financing** ([Part 23](./nvidia-huawei-deux-empires.md)). NVIDIA invests in clients (OpenAI, CoreWeave, xAI) who buy its chips with that money, which inflates its revenue and supports the price of its assets. Plus revenue guarantees (backstops) and GPU-backed debt. A structural analogy with the vendor financing of the 2000 telecom bubble (Cisco/Nortel). *Verdict: not illegal, but undisclosed, and at the heart of the bubble fear.*

**d/acc** (defensive/decentralized accelerationism, Buterin, [Part 19](./troisiemes-voies-ia.md)). A third way born of crypto: accelerate, but favoring defensive and decentralized technologies (that protect individuals) over offensive and centralizing ones. Against blind e/acc AND against the doomer moratorium. *Verdict: the most articulate of the third ways, remains a niche milieu.*

**Fascization and decomputing** (McQuillan, [Part 19](./troisiemes-voies-ia.md)). AI does not "become" fascist, it operates a fascization through its ordinary uses: sorting, exclusion, algorithmic states of exception, the production of statistical scapegoats. "Decomputing" is the inverse proposal: remove computation where it harms, rather than optimize it. *Verdict: an operational anti-fascist frame, completes the anatomy of Part 13.*

**Eugenics 2.0** (liberal eugenics, [Part 28](./eugenisme-tescreal-filiation.md)). Historical eugenics was state-run and coercive. Its 2026 version is private, voluntary, and market-based: preimplantation embryo selection by polygenic scores, sold as "giving the best start." The coercion has disappeared, the genetic sorting remains. *Verdict: the denial ("without state coercion, it isn't eugenics") is the mechanism itself.*

## 7. Summary Table: Bullshit or Not?

| Concept | Valid core | "Bullshit" drift | Overall verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Transhumanism | Augmented medicine, life extension | Immortality, upload, cryo: unproven faith, elite fantasy | Ideology, not science |
| Rationalism | Biases, probabilities, demand for evidence | Arrogance, delusional quantified certainties, sectarian culture | Valid tools turned into dogma |
| Accelerationism/e/acc | Technology really transforms the world | From fact (it accelerates) to duty (we must accelerate), an interested sophism | Fatalism disguised as philosophy |
| Technocracy | Expertise should feed the decision | Expertise replaces the vote, a denial of democracy | Dangerous when it short-circuits the people |
| Longtermism (Part 11) | Thinking long-term is healthy | Sacrificing the present to 10^58 future lives | Elitist, authoritarian implications |
| Vectofascism | Names a genuinely new power | Unfalsifiable ("I am not here" as proof) | Strong concept, safeguard needed |
| Circular financing | Describes a real mechanism | Over-read into strict equivalence with 2000 | Real systemic risk, partial analogy |
| Eugenics 2.0 | Fertility technology exists | "Without state coercion, no eugenics," the denial | Documented fascist lineage (Part 28) |

> **Reading rule:** throughout this galaxy, the form is scientific, the substance is often a faith. People who present themselves as "the ultra-rational" arrive at extreme and unverifiable conclusions (immortality, certain extinction, "only the enlightened should decide"). This is not total bullshit, it is an ideology that borrows the clothes of reason. This is exactly the critique of [Part 1](./manifeste-techno-optimiste-andreessen.md): technological reason turned into religion.
