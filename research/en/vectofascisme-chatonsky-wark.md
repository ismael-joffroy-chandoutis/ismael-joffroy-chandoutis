# Vectofascism: The Concept the Dossier Was Missing? (Chatonsky, after Wark)

> Reading and conceptual analysis report. Written June 5, 2026. Sources at the end of the document.
> Part 24: Part 13 dissected "techno-fascism" and concluded on its limits; Part 19 introduced McQuillan (fascization through AI). This part examines the field's most radical proposal: Grégory Chatonsky's **vectofascism**, which holds that the very concept of technofascism is conceptual laziness, and that the mutation runs deeper: it is not fascists who use technology, it is the mathematical properties of vector spaces that are reconfiguring the political.

## 1. Genealogy: McKenzie Wark's vectoralist class

The concept does not come out of nowhere. In *A Hacker Manifesto* (2004) and then *Capital is Dead: Is This Something Worse?* (2019), McKenzie Wark theorizes the **vectoralist class**: those who own not the means of production but the *vectors* of information, the infrastructures through which information circulates, aggregates and is valorized (platforms, networks, databases, and now models). Facing it, the "hacker class": those who produce new information but do not own the vectors. Wark's provocation: if the ruling class extracts its power from informational asymmetry rather than from classic wage exploitation, then this is no longer exactly capitalism, it is "something worse," a regime our Marxist categories do not yet name. Applied to the dossier: NVIDIA, the hyperscalers and the labs (Parts 16, 20, 23) are not industrialists but vectoralists, they own the directions, the intensities and the conditions of circulation of all information.

Chatonsky extends and displaces this: "where Wark diagnosed the emergence of a new capitalist formation, I observe its crystallization into a specific **political regime**." Wark's vectoralist class remained "fully phenomenal", locatable, nameable, attackable. Vectofascism, by contrast, "operates through spectrality": it draws its efficacy from "its capacity to deny its own existence while exerting its effects."

## 2. The critique of "technofascism": why Part 13 is not enough

The text "From technofascism to vectofascism" is a frontal attack on this dossier's keyword, and it deserves to be taken seriously:

1. **Conceptual laziness**: "technofascism" maintains a "separation between technics (means) and fascism (end)", it presupposes a neutral technology hijacked by bad actors. That is "augmented fascism, but it is not a qualitative mutation of the political form itself." Yet all the dossier's material (flood the zone, epistemic bubbles, algorithmic modulation) suggests precisely a mutation of form, not a mere amplification.
2. **Hidden Heideggerianism**: the concept perpetuates an essentialist vision of technics as ontological threat. Consequence: "politically paralyzing", "when everything becomes technofascist, nothing really is in any specific way." This is exactly the conclusion Part 13 reached by another route (the word mobilizes but does not discriminate).
3. **The decisive displacement**: the Dark Enlightenment (Parts 3, 7) is not "simply a reactionary ideology that would use technology", it "emerges from the very properties of computational vector spaces." Example given: when Thiel declares freedom and democracy incompatible (Part 2), this is not an ideological preference but "the formalization of a mathematical relation between variables in an optimization space." The "Freedom Cities" (Part 9) are not fleeing the state: they are "redefining architecture according to a computational logic."

## 3. The concept: definition and mechanisms

Chatonsky's synthetic definition: "a political system characterized by the algorithmic instrumentalization of information flows and digital spaces to produce and orient collective affects." The mechanisms, which form a system:

- **From masses to vectors.** Historical fascism operated "in three-dimensional Euclidean space" (rallies, uniforms, bodies); vectofascism "operates in an n-dimensional hyperspace where the very notion of a 'gathering' becomes obsolete." Ideological inscription on bodies becomes "algorithmic modulation of flows of attention and affect", a "cognitive orthopedics that no longer applies to muscles but to synapses." "The fascist binary order/disorder is replaced by the binary **signal/noise**."
- **The leader as interface.** The cult of the leader persists but changes function: "what is worshipped is not the leader's supposed depth but his capacity to function as a perfect projection surface." Grotesqueness (the orange makeup) is not a flaw but a *signal of belonging* to the regime of the technical image.
- **Calculated undecidability.** Post-truth is not lying but "the production of a grey zone where the very status of the statement becomes indeterminable", exploiting the fundamental economic asymmetry: producing an assertion costs seconds, verifying it costs hours. (Brandolini's law, industrialized.)
- **The personalized enemy.** Where historical fascism designated a single, stable scapegoat (the Jew, the communist), vectofascism "calculates personalized enemies for each node of the network", a "made-to-measure hatred, algorithmically optimized." Major political consequence: coherent opposition becomes structurally impossible, since each user perceives a slightly different power.
- **The industrialization of the differend.** A concept borrowed from Lyotard (the differend: a conflict no common tribunal can adjudicate, the parties sharing no regime of phrases). Vectofascism "actively produces this incommensurability": "algorithms create regimes of phrases tailored to each node of the network, making even awareness of the existence of a differend impossible." QAnon is not a conspiracy theory but "a complete informational ecosystem" with its own criteria of truth.
- **Vectorial de-semanticization.** In an LLM's latent space, "democracy" becomes a "4096-dimensional vector none of whose dimensions can be directly translated into familiar conceptual terms"; the "distance" between democracy and fascism is measured there "by cosine similarity metrics," not politically. Information ceases to be semantic use-value and becomes computational exchange-value.
- **Spectrality.** "Vectofascism is all the more effective in that it can always say: 'I am not here.'" Algorithms are "specters: invisible to the users they modulate, present only through their effects." Hence the structural denial: "This is not fascism," says the system, while executing its mechanisms.
- **The invisibilized material base.** Chatonsky insists: this spectral power rests on "the intensive extraction of energy and mineral resources", the explicit junction with Parts 16 and 22: "the same process of accumulation runs through all registers of existence" (exploited bodies, extracted resources, captured subjectivities).

## 4. The vectofascist aesthetic: from marble to kitsch

The aesthetic component of the concept ("The vectofascist aesthetic") inverts the expected iconography: historical fascism wanted "a monumental classicism celebrating the grandiose ruins of a mythified past" (Speer, Marinetti, Part 6); contemporary fascism adopts "the grotesque, kitsch, and an appropriation of pop culture", excess, referential overload, "a deliberate vulgarity whose humor is as if contained within resentment." Pepe memes rather than bronze eagles.

- **Generative AI as the regime's aesthetic machine**: neural networks *naturally* produce referential accumulation, the 4chan aesthetic matches the statistical workings of the models. Saturation overwhelms critical mechanisms.
- **The Beeple case**: Mike Winkelmann's work, excessively grotesque, is not a critique but "an integral relativism in which distinctions of value become impossible", vulgar humor as a "deactivation of judgment." And the troubling coincidence: this aesthetic leveling *converges* with the mathematical foundation of AI, "where all data are reduced to vectors comparable according to purely statistical criteria."
- **Reversibility (chirality)**: contents become "bifid", a critical video on Gaza repurposed by Trump as a promotional clip. The ambivalence is not an accident: it is the condition of the "progressive rehabilitation of unacceptable political positions."
- **The proposed way out**: Chatonsky opposes "factual fascism" (referential circularity, the "nauseating repetition of the already-known" that naturalizes the existing order) to an emancipatory "**factitious aesthetic**": exploring "zones of indeterminacy, generative accidents," deliberately integrating "a share of noise," assuming the simulacrum as simulacrum in order to keep "open the possibility of other worlds." Latent space as an aesthetic battlefield, not refusing AI, but refusing its default referential regime.

## 5. The concept tested against the dossier

Confrontation with the material accumulated since Part 1:

| Fact documented in the dossier | Technofascist reading (Part 13) | Vectofascist reading |
|---|---|---|
| "Flood the zone with shit" (Bannon) | Amplified propaganda | Industrialization of the differend, the production of incommensurability is the *product*, not a side effect |
| Thiel: "freedom and democracy incompatible" | Neo-reactionary ideology | Output of an optimization calculation: democracy as an unsatisfiable constraint |
| The word "safety" erased from the vocabulary in 18 months (Part 17) | Administrative censorship | Modulation of the semantic field, the concept is not repressed, it is de-vectorized |
| Personalized enemies in the feeds (X, recommendation) | Mass manipulation | Made-to-measure hatred: there is no longer a "mass" to manipulate, only nodes |
| Grok/Mechahitler, presidential AI memes | Slip-ups | The regime's aesthetic: the grotesque as a signal of belonging |
| The opposition fragmented, unable to name the adversary | Conjunctural political weakness | Structural effect: each node perceives a different power, the differend is industrialized |
| The permanent denial ("this isn't fascism, look: no militias, no single party") | Definitional debate (Paxton/Eco) | Spectrality IS the mechanism: a regime whose denial is part of its functioning |

The complementarity with the dossier's other concepts takes shape as follows: **Coeckelbergh/Part 13** describes the alliance (who governs with what); **McQuillan/Part 19** describes the social process (fascization through uses: sorting, exclusion, algorithmic states of exception); **Chatonsky** describes the *milieu* (the properties of the space in which the political now takes place). The three levels, alliance, process, milieu, do not compete; they nest.

## 6. Critical reading: strengths and flaws of the concept

**What the concept genuinely brings:**
1. It explains what "technofascism" does not: why *this* fascism needs neither masses, nor a party, nor a project, nor even to know itself as fascist. The Paxton/Eco/Stanley grids (Part 13) failed on the absence of mass mobilization; vectofascism makes that its defining trait.
2. It is the only concept in the field that takes **mathematical materiality** seriously: latent space is not a metaphor there but the actual site (de-semanticization, cosine similarity, n-dimensionality). For an artistic practice that works latent space as material, it is the only theoretical framework that speaks of the real object.
3. It integrates aesthetics as constitutive rather than decorative, and it offers a program (the factitious aesthetic) rather than a diagnosis alone.

**The flaws, to be documented with the same honesty:**
1. **The risk of unfalsifiability.** A regime that is "all the more effective in that it can say 'I am not here'" is a regime in which the absence of proof becomes proof. Structurally, this is the self-sealing argument we reproached Yudkowsky for in Part 14 (disagreement confirms the thesis). Spectrality is a powerful intuition and a methodological trap.
2. **Inverted determinism.** Saying that the Dark Enlightenment "emerges from the very properties of vector spaces" risks replacing one essentialism (the Heideggerian technics-as-threat, rightly criticized) with another (mathematics as political destiny). The same vector spaces run GraphCast and hate recommendation; the mathematical property does not explain the political capture, capital and power do. Wark, by sticking to class analysis, retains an advantage here: his vectoralist class has names, addresses and balance sheets (Parts 8, 23). Chatonsky's specter does not.
3. **The absence of a theory of action.** Like the technofascism it criticizes, vectofascism describes magnificently and arms little: "how to resist what operates in dimensions we cannot perceive?" is a real question, but the dossier's answers (Parts 21, 25, unions, moratoriums, antitrust, taxes) all operate in good old Euclidean space, and some of them win. The European platform directive *did* seize the algorithm through labor law. The differend is perhaps not so industrialized that no common tribunal subsists.
4. **Status of the corpus**: texts by an artist-theorist published on his site and in a journal (carrier-bag.net), not yet stabilized by critical academic reception, to be treated as a living concept, not as settled knowledge. That is also its strength: it thinks in real time what the university will take ten years to name.

**Working position for the dossier**: adopt the triplet **alliance (Coeckelbergh) / process (McQuillan) / milieu (Chatonsky-Wark)** as the complete grid, reserving "vectofascism" for the phenomena the other two levels do not explain (personalization of the enemy, calculated undecidability, aesthetics of the grotesque, structural denial). And keep Wark's vectoralist class as a reminder that behind the specter, there are owners.
