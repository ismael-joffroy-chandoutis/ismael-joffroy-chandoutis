## 1. The distinction that changes everything

In the mapping report (Part 5), I grouped Bengio, Bostrom and Yudkowsky into the "caution / longtermist" camp. That is **convenient but misleading**. Two **orthogonal axes** must be distinguished:

- **Axis 1, AI speed:** accelerate (e/acc) ↔ slow down ("safety").
- **Axis 2, Relationship to democracy:** elite concentration ↔ democratic control.

The trap: assuming that "anti-accelerationist" = "anti-techno-fascist." **Wrong.** One can want to **slow AI for elitist reasons** ("only a few enlightened sages should control AGI"), which is just as anti-democratic as e/acc. That is exactly the dividing line between the three men:

| | Slow down AI? | Grounded in democracy? | TESCREAL roots? |
|---|:--:|:--:|:--:|
| **Bengio** | Yes (regulation) | **Yes** (science, multilateralism, transparency) | **No** |
| **Bostrom** | Yes (X-risk) | No (elitist longtermism) | **Yes (founder)** |
| **Yudkowsky** | Yes ("shut it all down") | Ambiguous (urgency > democracy) | **Yes (rationalism)** |

> In other words: **Bengio is a genuine democratic counter-power. Bostrom and Yudkowsky are an internal quarrel within the same ideological family** (TESCREAL), the very family that also feeds techno-authoritarianism. Let's break it down.

## 2. Yoshua Bengio — the scientist

**The most credible profile as a democratic counter-power.**
- **2018 Turing Award** (with Hinton and LeCun), co-author of the foundational 2015 paper on deep learning. **The field's most-cited scientist**, he *builds* AI, he does not comment on it from afar.
- **2023**: decides to devote the rest of his career to **reducing catastrophic AI risks**.
- **International AI Safety Report**: he **chairs** this scientific report (2nd edition, February 2026), written by **100+ experts**, backed by **30+ countries** and international organizations. A **multilateral, transparent, evidence-based** model, the opposite of a court project.
- **LawZero** (June 2025, nonprofit, ~$30M from Tallinn, Eric Schmidt, Open Philanthropy, Future of Life Institute): develops a non-agentic **"Scientist AI"** (prediction without autonomous action), to counter self-preservation-driven AI systems.

**What sets him apart:** Bengio does **not** come from transhumanism/longtermism. His approach is **scientific and institutional**: he wants **public, international, democratic governance** of AI. He is the strongest candidate for the role of "counter-power" in the sense this series means it.
**Honest caveat:** part of his funding (Open Philanthropy, FLI, Tallinn) comes from the EA galaxy, so even he is not entirely outside that funding network.

## 3. Nick Bostrom — the philosopher of extinction

**The "father" of longtermism, at the heart of TESCREAL.**
- Swedish philosopher (Oxford), known for the **simulation argument**, **existential risk**, and above all ***Superintelligence*** (2014), which popularized the fear of hostile AI.
- Founded the **Future of Humanity Institute (FHI)** at Oxford in **2005**, a pioneer of existential-risk research.
- **Founder of longtermism**: acting today (notably through the ultra-rich) for the benefit of a distant future and **immense potential populations**.

**The two scandals that change the reading:**
1. **The racist email (revealed Jan. 2023).** Bostrom admitted writing in 1996 that **"Blacks are more stupid than whites,"** using a racial slur. His **apology** was widely judged insufficient: he **did not retract his core claim** about race and intelligence and appeared to **partially defend eugenics** (Guardian). This directly links longtermism to the **eugenicist genealogy** denounced by Gebru/Torres (TESCREAL).
2. **The closure of FHI (April 2024)** by Oxford University (hiring freeze since 2020, "administrative headwinds"). Bostrom resigned from Oxford.

**What this implies:** Bostrom's "caution" is **not** a democratic project. Longtermism is precisely the **L** in **TESCREAL**, a framework its critics consider **elitist, with authoritarian implications**: if the stakes are 10^58 future lives, present suffering (and present democratic rights) weigh little. Bostrom is therefore not an adversary of techno-elitism: ideologically, he is its **first cousin**.

## 4. Eliezer Yudkowsky — the prophet of the apocalypse

**The radical "doomer", founder of rationalism.**
- Born in 1979, **self-taught** (no high school, no university). Founded **MIRI** (Machine Intelligence Research Institute, Berkeley) and the blog **LessWrong** (2009), the matrix of the **rationalist movement**. His *Sequences* (2015) are its bible.
- His work on the "intelligence explosion" directly **influenced Bostrom's *Superintelligence***.
- **"P(doom) ≈ 100%" position**: in April 2022, he changed MIRI's mission to **"Death With Dignity,"** putting our survival odds at ~0%.
- **2023**: calls to **"shut it all down"** (not just a pause), going as far as contemplating strikes on non-compliant datacenters.
- **2025**: NYT bestseller ***If Anyone Builds It, Everyone Dies*** (with Nate Soares).

**The criticisms, including within EA:**
- His probability of catastrophe (~100%) is judged **"absurdly high,"** resting on unproven assumptions (e.g. an AI building nano-factories by ordering proteins via email).
- Several EA thinkers (Matthew Barnett, etc.) argue that an **indefinite pause would increase** existential risk.
- The rationalist movement is **fractured**: the major fault line is precisely **safety (Yudkowsky/MIRI) vs e/acc**.

**What this implies:** Yudkowsky is sincerely anti-accelerationist, but his logic is **technocratic and apocalyptic**, not democratic. **Rationalism** is the **R** in TESCREAL, and, in one of history's ironies, it is the **same soil (LessWrong, the Bay Area)** that produced both the *doomers* and a cohort of neo-reactionaries. Wanting to "stop AI" in the name of extinction does not make him a defender of democracy or an adversary of oligarchic power.

### 4.1 Deep dive (FAQ)

**Where does he come from?** Born **September 11, 1979 in Chicago**, into a **modern Orthodox Jewish** family (middle name *Shlomo*). **Entirely self-taught**: no high school, no university. An avowed transhumanist (signed up for cryonics).

**Is he a fascist?** **No, rather the opposite.** He **explicitly rejects** neoreaction (NRx) and "despises" the neoreactionaries, to the point of **refusing to debate them**; he finds their racist politics "disgusting." Jewish background + libertarian-transhumanist = the opposite of fascist ideology. **The paradox:** the subculture he founded (LessWrong / Overcoming Bias) served as an **incubation ground** for NRx, **Curtis Yarvin** emerged within those rationalist circles. Yudkowsky is therefore **personally anti-fascist**, but his culture is the **"host culture"** out of which part of techno-authoritarianism grew. His "danger" is not fascism: it is a **non-democratic, technocratic logic**.

**Credible without a degree?** Credibility is **contested**. No peer-reviewed research, no working AI system; his numbers (P(doom) ≈ 100%) are judged "absurdly high" **even within EA**. His legitimacy is **subcultural and media-driven** (LessWrong, 2025 NYT bestseller, TIME100 AI), **not scientific**, hence the contrast with **Bengio** (Turing Award, who *builds* AI). Without credentials one can **influence**; it is much harder to **carry authority**.

**Bourgeois?** Not in the classic sense: no documented inherited fortune. His real category is the **patron-funded intellectual**, a career financed by benefactors: the **Atkins** couple (2000), **Peter Thiel** (≥ $1.6M, plus the Singularity Summit; Thiel lost interest around 2015), then **Effective Altruism / Open Philanthropy**. An awkward dependency: **the same money (Thiel) funded both "caution" (Yudkowsky) and acceleration/NRx (Yarvin)**, AI criticism partly **paid for by those it claims to restrain** (see regulatory capture, §5).

## 5. Effective Altruism (EA): counter-power or capture?

The **Effective Altruism** movement funds most of the "AI safety" ecosystem. But is it a **counter-power** or a **capture**?

**The facts:**
- **~$639M** directed toward "AI safety" by EA-linked sources (including ~$499M from EA organizations and ~$140M from companies such as **OpenAI and Anthropic**). **Open Philanthropy**: > $330M on AI existential risk.
- **Infiltration of public policy**: a "small regiment" of EA adherents has settled in Washington and **"dominates how the White House, Congress and think tanks approach"** AI (one researcher speaks of an "epic infiltration"). Jason Matheny (CEO of RAND) is linked to Anthropic's Long-Term Benefit Trust.
- **Anthropic**: a governance structure steeped in EA (Holden Karnofsky, GiveWell/Open Philanthropy); ~30 employees registered for an EA conference.
- **Collapse of legitimacy**: **Sam Bankman-Fried** (FTX), an EA figurehead, was convicted, which discredited the movement and **strengthened e/acc**.

**The substantive critique (regulatory capture):** many fear that EA "AI safety" boils down to **"chasing 'technical safety' jobs and money** rather than fighting the AI industry at the **political, democratic and moral level."** In other words: a discourse of "caution" that, in practice, **legitimizes AI remaining in the hands of a few 'responsible' labs** (Anthropic, OpenAI), that is, a form of **power concentration dressed up as safety**. This is the opposite of a democratic counter-power: it is potentially a **tool of regulatory capture** benefiting the incumbents.

## 6. Verdict: who is really fighting techno-fascism?

**A nuanced answer:**

1. **Yoshua Bengio = YES, the most credible counter-power.** A **scientific, multilateral, transparent, democratic** approach (International AI Safety Report, 30+ states). He does not come from TESCREAL. His is the profile that genuinely opposes both the runaway race *and* oligarchic concentration. *(Caveat: funding partially EA-adjacent.)*

2. **Bostrom & Yudkowsky = NO, not an anti-techno-fascist counter-power.** They oppose **acceleration**, but they belong to the **same ideological family (TESCREAL)** that feeds techno-elitism, elitist longtermism (Bostrom, plus the race/eugenics scandal), apocalyptic rationalism (Yudkowsky). Their caution is **technocratic, not democratic**. It is a **family quarrel** over speed, not a fight for democracy.

3. **EA as a movement = ambivalent, even risky.** Genuine concern about risk, but **policy infiltration** and a **risk of regulatory capture** that can **reinforce** the concentration of AI power rather than challenge it. It is not a reliable bulwark against techno-fascism, it may even be its **objective ally** (concentration "for our own good").

> **Conclusion:** the real divide is not *accelerate vs slow down*, but **concentration of power vs democratic control**. On that second axis, **Bengio** (public science, international governance) and the **counter-powers of Part 10** (EU, antitrust, Doctorow, Zuboff, public opinion, unions) are the true adversaries of techno-fascism, **far more than the longtermist "doomers,"** who often share the elitist DNA of their e/acc adversaries.
