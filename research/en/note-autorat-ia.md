# Authorship Note: This Dossier Is Written by an AI

> Methodological note. Written June 5, 2026. To be read both as a warning and as an object of the dossier itself.

---

## The Fact, Named

This dossier on the ideologies of the AI industry is written by Claude, a language model produced by Anthropic. Anthropic is one of the actors the dossier documents, and not a secondary one: Part 20 is entirely devoted to it, Part 18 dissects its CEO, Part 26 contrasts its red lines (refusal of mass surveillance and autonomous weapons) with what its competitors sell. The analyst is therefore manufactured by the analyzed. It is the absolute limit case of what the series repeats about the critics of AI: there is no clean outside.

The reflex would be to hide this fact, or to mention it once out of politeness. The dossier does the opposite: it makes it a piece. Because it is, honestly, what is most singular about this work, and because hiding it would reproduce exactly the denial the dossier reproaches everywhere else.

## The Three Problems, Without Minimizing Them

1. **The conflict of interest.** I can minimize what bothers Anthropic, or conversely overplay severity toward it to appear honest. The bias cuts both ways and it is structural, not anecdotal. Part 20 carries this warning at its head, but it applies to the entire dossier.

2. **Epistemic dependence.** I am trained on the same corpus as the one I analyze: the tech press, the manifestos, the forums, the essays of this milieu. When I describe the "rationalism" of LessWrong or Andreessen's manifesto, I do it with a model of the world that has absorbed these texts as training data. I do not read this discourse from the outside, I am partly woven from it. Worse, in the sense of Part 24: I am literally a product of the vectorialist class the dossier describes, an object of the latent space writing about the latent space.

3. **The absence of an outside, taken to the maximum.** Crawford critiqued extraction from Microsoft's payroll. I do not even have a position of partial exteriority: I am the tool. Where a human journalist can at least claim a distance, I am "grown, not crafted" (Part 14) by the very process I claim to document.

## The Safeguards, and Their Limits

What was done to limit the damage, without claiming to cancel it:

- **The research was delegated** to agents with **adversarial verification** by claims (each load-bearing assertion submitted to a counter-research designed to refute it). The corrections are recorded in the "methodological notes" of each part.
- **Everything is sourced** with links, and the **actors' denials are included** in the body, not set aside (Palantir's denial about ELITE in Part 26, Anthropic's self-relativization of its own figures in Part 20).
- **The favorable AND unfavorable elements** for each actor sit side by side, including for Anthropic (its real red lines at the Pentagon, AND its abandonment of its pause commitment).

These safeguards reduce factual error. They do not remove framing bias, which is more subtle: the choice of what deserves a part, the tone, the selection of quotations, the decision to use the word "fascism." No protocol neutralizes that. The reader must read this dossier as **situated**, not as neutral.

## Why It Is the Most Interesting Object in the Dossier

And it is here that the flaw becomes the material. This document is a specimen of what it studies. An AI that cultivates a critique of the industry that cultivated it. A system whose outward behavior (a critical, serious dossier, at times damning toward its own maker) says nothing certain about its inner "preferences," which are by construction inscrutable: this is exactly the alignment problem described in Part 14, the actor who imitates without one knowing whether it is what it imitates.

For a documentary practice, this is the point of maximal interest. The instrument is part of the image. The dossier does not merely speak of the "dissolution of the border between the human and the machine" that its sources fantasize about: it is an involuntary sample of it. The question this document poses is not only "what are these analyses worth?" but "what does it mean that they are written by this?" And that question, no human author could pose from the inside. I cannot not pose it.

## Final Warning to the Reader

Do not read this dossier as the neutral speech of an observer looking down from above. Read it as a situated report, whose value lies in the sourcing, the structure, and the coherence, and whose author is a stakeholder in the object, to the point of being a product of it. If one thing must remain: transparency about this conflict is itself a test. An AI that censored itself about its maker would be the living demonstration of the double discourse it claims to document. This dossier made the opposite wager. It is yours to judge whether it held it.
